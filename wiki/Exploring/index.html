Exploring

From The Dominion Encyclopedia
Jump to: navigation, search

Exploring is quite simply to play an entire round without training any offensive units but rather just train defensive units and explore for land instead of invade. An explorer aims to play as efficiently as possible, trying to compete with attackers and other explorers for highest land.

Race Selection

All races can explore, but some are better and more fit than others. There is essentially only one race that cannot explore and that is Orc, since their defense relies on prestige. It is possible to acquire full or nearly full prestige and explore from there, but then it is no longer exploring per se but rather a form of converting.

Concerning Kobold, Goblin and Lycanthrope, see fast exploring.

Building Selection

This depends on race and how the player wishes to play the race. Races that are not depend on a specific landtype or building can be played in a myriad of ways. There is no single way that is better than the other, but it is common for explorers to (unlike attackers) not run many Smithies. A common thing to do is to leave protection with max Smithies and then never build anymore; some may even destroy them and use the land to build something else.

Land-dependent races offer little strategy variance but are stable and solid as they are.

Explorers often play with medium to very high castle improvements. Teching is sometimes utilized.

Unit Selection

Explorers aim to play as efficiently as possible and do as such try to keep spec units to a minimum and train nearly exclusively elites. While spec units offer speed, elites are much more efficient (see Fast exploring). While Wood Elves for instance have a 4 DP spec unit, they should try to avoid training a lot of these as they are not as efficient as their elite DP unit.

General information

Explorers are often criticized for being boring and having no skill, playing a way that demands next to nothing from the player. Explorers often defend themselves by arguing that they are free to do as they wish and that exploring indeed requires skills and planning. It is however a fact that a lot of the land attackers gain comes from explorers.

Costs

Exploration comes at the cost of platinum and draftees. The draftees used to pay for explored land return as peasants over the following hours. The platinum cost is decided by the following formula: 

1.1 * (1000 + 3*(Land-300) ^ 1.09)

As of Round 64 and the subsequent rounds (which includes all other rulesets), exploring costs is now 10% more and the exploring cost bumps have been removed.

Limit

To throttle mass exploration, there is a limit which prevents dominions more than 500 acres large to explore more than 50% their current size in 12 hours. The limit for dominions below 500 acres is 100%. For example, a dominion that is 1000 acres cannot have more than 500 explored acres incoming until it has increased in size, while the limit for a dominion that is 400 acres is 400 acres.

Pros

  • A good way to get to know the game and its dynamics.
  • Explorers can train massive spies and wizards and help attackers with ops.

Cons

  • Likely to get boring.

Recommendations
