Units

From The Dominion Encyclopedia

Jump to: navigation, search

Your dominion must have an army, which in turn consists of units. There are two classes of units: Military and Spies and Wizards. Note that the game considers all units to be a part of your military.

Military

Every race has five types of military units; four unique and all races have draftees.

  • Draftees: All draftees give 1 DP each, at the cost of a peasant.
  • 1st specialist: For most races, the 1st specialist has 3 OP although some have 4 and some have less than 3.
  • 2nd specialist: This is the defensive specialist. Most have 3 DP, some have 4 and some have less than 3.
  • 1st elite: For all races except Merfolk, Halfling, Goblin and Troll, this is the elite defender. (Goblin has no elite defender.)
  • 2nd elite: With the same exceptions as for 1st elite, but these are the elite attackers. Trolls will want to train nothing but 2nd elites (Smashers) as soon as their income allows them to.

Discussion

Generally speaking, using elites is considered more efficient while using specialists (specs) is considered fast but ineffficient. There is no right way to play Dominion and it is as such not wrong to use specs if that is what works out the best. Races with 4 point specs often use them although Wood Elves are rarely seen with many Longbowmen as they aren't considered worthwhile to train beyond a bare minimum. Spirits and Undeads rarely use 1st specialists but are often seen with a number of 2nd specialists to speed up the DP.

Spies and Wizards

Spies and wizards are necessary in an army.

  • Spy: Crucial unit for espionage for all races except Halfling and Lizardfolk.
  • Wizard: Crucial unit for offensive spells. Not required to cast self spells. Pointless for Dark Elf and Sylvan.
  • ArchMages: twice as strong as wizards and immortal (can neither be assassinated nor die on failed spells).

Discussion

It is debatable whether ArchMages (AMs) are worthwhile or not. The complete cost (without any bonuses) for an AM is 1500 platinum (500 for the Wizard and 1000 for the AM), but they are as strong as two wizards and only use up one draftee (and thus only one peasant) each, making them more cost efficient than wizards after about 13-14 days.

Training

In order to obtain units, they must first be trained. Units are trained from draftees, which are gained from peasants. The only exception to this are ArchMages, which are trained from wizards.

Time

All units, except for specialists, take 12 hours to train. Specialists take 9 hours to train. ArchMages effectively take 24 hours to train.

Cost

Unit costs may vary depending on ruleset. Costs may be reduced by three factors: Smithies, techs and smith heroes. All bonuses are multiplicative with each other, making the unit cost formula as follows: 

 (Unit cost) * (1 - Smithy bonus) * (1 - Tech bonus) * (1 - Hero bonus)

It is common practice for attackers to run max smithies to pay as little as possible for their units. The usability for Gnomes is argued. While they do not lower the ore costs for Gnomes, they lower the platinum cost and it is argued that this bonus helps, while the opposition argues that Gnomish units already have low platinum costs and that one is better off building something else instead of smithies.

Icekin

To be able to afford the high WPA an attacking or converting Icekin needs, they have a racial bonus that reduces the cost of ArchMages by 150 platinum (which effectively is a 15% discount; regular wizards still cost the same). On top off this, Icekins commonly choose to tech and obtain the tech that reduces the cost of wizards by 20%. This makes wizards cost 400 platinum and ArchMages cost 680 platinum, making the total cost of an ArchMage 1080 platinum. 

(1000-150) * 0.8 = 680
