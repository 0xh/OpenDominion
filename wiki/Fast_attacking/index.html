Fast attacking

From The Dominion Encyclopedia
Jump to: navigation, search

Fast attacking is a common strategy in Dominion. The objective is to gain as much land as possible in a very short period of time. This can be achieved through race selection, choice of buildings and choice of units. Fast attacking provides the benefits of speed, but comes at the cost of efficiency. Although many round winners have been fast attackers, it is a very risky strategy, as inefficiency can be a tremendous hindrance late round.

Race Selection

A number of races are considered fast due to their racial bonuses and types of units available to train. Typically, a fast race will either have a population bonus or cheap, yet inefficient units. The following races are considered fast:

Good

  • Dwarf
  • Firewalker
  • Halfling
  • Spirit
  • Kobold* (*Kobold is only available as a good race during the Tech ruleset)

Evil

  • Goblin
  • Kobold
  • Lizardfolk
  • Lycanthrope
  • Orc
  • Undead


Building Selection

A fast strategy usually requires a lot of "mod" buildings as well as alchemies. Mod buildings give bonuses to troops to make them more effective sooner, giving them increased military power relative to an efficient dominion. Mod buildings and alchemies, however, do not provide a long-term advantage. Population-oriented and investment buildings such as Homes, Barracks and Diamond Mines are very beneficial late in the round when raw military numbers are high. One's population with a fast strategy, however, would be significantly lower. A typical fast build includes large numbers of some of the following:

Build

  • Alchemies
  • Guard Towers
  • Gryphon Nests
  • Temples
  • Shrines


Unit Selection

Fast strategies generally involve the heavy use of spec units instead of elite units. Most of the time, a player using a fast strategy will not use only spec units, but instead will choose a combination of either spec OP and elite DP or spec DP and elite OP units. This differs from slower strategies where a player will train elite units almost exclusively.

Goblins could be considered the ultimate fast race, as their main defensive unit is their spec. This is because neither of their elite units have more defensive power than the spec and therefore, it would be a waste of platinum to train an elite unit in place of the spec for defense.

Many Lycanthrope attackers also use a speed strategy, making use of their extremely cheap defensive spec while training mainly elite OP units. It makes sense to train the defensive spec over the elite spec as they are 3 and 4 points on DP respectively, yet have a substantial difference in cost of training.

Suggested Unit Mixes

  • Dwarf - Spec DP, Elite OP
  • Firewalker - Spec DP, Elite OP
  • Halfling - Spec DP, Elite OP
  • Spirit - *some* Spec DP, Elite OP
  • Goblin - Spec DP, Elite OP
  • Lizardfolk - Spec DP, Elite OP
  • Lycanthrope - Spec DP, Elite OP
  • Orc - Orcs are unique in that both spec OP and DP can be used for speed with both elite OP and DP
  • Undead - *some* spec DP, Elite OP


General information

It is not only fast races that can be used for a fast strategy. Slower, more efficient races can be played in a fast way that will keep up with the top dominions. However, these races are not very likely to "run away". It is not necessary to use a fast race with a fast build and use specs. A different combination of the 3 can be used and still be effective in playing a fast strategy.

During the so called Imp ruleset, elite prices are increased and smithies are weakened, making the use of specs a lot more viable. Nearly all of the leading contenders in the Imp ruleset rounds use fast strategies and make use of lots of specs.

Pros

  • Most round winners recently have used a fast strategy
  • Fast strategies will give a better chance of playing at the top level early on in the round
  • Many players consider this strategy to be the most fun

Cons

  • Very few players will finish on high land with a fast strategy
  • Using a fast strategy will severely damage your dominion mid-late round due to a small population and very high draft rate
  • Fast strategies usually fail when played by inexperienced players

Recommendations

Those playing fast strategies are usually either:

  • Experienced players
  • People playing "for fun"
