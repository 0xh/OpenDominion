Firewalker is one of the fast attacking good races. They have a few interesting abilities that make them a fun, albeit fairly weak race to play. Although they are a solid race to attack OOP due to their cheap units and Alchemist Flame spell, they are very weak later on due to their inefficiency. Firewalkers have one of the most interesting self spells. The Alchemist Flame spell increases the amount of platinum produced by alchemies which means that unlike most other races, a high alchemy strategy is very viable for Firewalkers (especially explorers). This is of particular benefit if someone wishes to play fast. It is fairly uncommon for people to play as an exploring Firewalker; however, when experienced players have done so, their results have been very good. Generally, Firewalkers will only do well when in the hands of an experienced player, whether attacking or exploring.

Tech rounds

During the Tech ruleset, Firewalkers gain a slight boost to their OP elites. Salamanders gain an offensive boost depending upon their targets constructed forest buildings if their target is Evil. This is particularly strong against Orcs because they have their homes on forests. It is fairly common when there are strong Firewalkers in the Tech ruleset, that many Evil players will not build any lumberyards in order to lower the Firewalker elite OP. Furthermore, the Phoenix gains 0.5 raw DP and becomes a 5 DP unit at relatively the same cost (from 925p to 1025p as per the Round 55 changes).

The Firewalker racial spell is reduced from producing 15 to 10 more platinum per alchemy. This mostly affects exploring Firewalkers.

Imp rounds

In the Imp Ruleset, Firewalkers are boosted slightly once more. Due to their reasonably cheap OP, they are affected slightly less than other races. Their reduced casualties on OP elites mean that the cost of replacing troops with the higher priced elites is much lower than other races. Firewalkers also gain an additional population bonus of 2.5% making their total bonus 7.5% in Imp rounds.

General information

Pros

  • Cheap OP elites
  • Immortal DP elites (except when invaded by Icekin)
  • 50% reduced casualties on OP elites
  • Solid turtle from OP elites
  • Can be played very fast
  • Good self spell for early round

Cons

  • Inefficient troops
  • Self spell becomes too expensive to use late round making high-alchemy strats inefficient
  • Too reliant upon speed and hard to play slow
  • Poor converters

Good for

  • Intermediate to experienced attackers
  • Experienced explorers
