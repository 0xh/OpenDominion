Population

From The Dominion Encyclopedia

Jump to: navigation, search

Population is the largest determinant of how your Dominion is doing throughout the round. Your population is made up of both peasants and military. All Homes house a RAW 30 people (peasants OR military) and all employment buildings house RAW 15 people. Barren land house either RAW 5 or RAW 10 people depending on the race. These numbers are all affected by population bonuses (see below). Barracks house 36 military only (not peasants) and are not affected by population bonuses.

Military

Your military is what determines whether you can defend your acres or invade other people in order to conquer their's (see Attacking). During the course of the round, your military population will gradually increase as your lands grow and a greater military presence is required in order to defend your lands and conquer others. Inefficient Races will have a much larger military population than more efficient races and will therefore usually receive a population bonus.

Peasants

Your peasant population is often overlooked by most inexperienced players. It is not written in the Scribes but Employed peasants produce 2.7 Platinum each every hour which is then multiplied by any platinum bonuses. Because of this, it is very important to have a large peasant population as the more platinum you produce, the more military you can train or acres you can explore.

Employment

Employment is determined by how many jobs your Dominion has compared to how many Peasants. Each non-population building employs 20 peasants whereas Homes and Barracks employ 0 peasants. The number of jobs your Dominion currently has is shown on the Statistics Advisor page. This also shows whether you have too many jobs (Job Overrun) or too few (Job Underrun). If you have jobs that need to be filled by peasants and have spare draftees, it is a good idea to release these excess draftees back into your peasant population. This is providing that you do not require the draftees for additional DP.

The only way to increase your number of jobs available without constructing more buildings is to own the Great Market Wonder.

Growth rate

The formula for population growth rate is as follows: 

(Current population) * (1 + (0.03 * (1 + Bonus/100)))

Bonuses are Temples, Harmony (+50%) and Kobold racial bonus.

For example, you are a non-Kobold race, you have 10,000 current total population, harmony (+50%), and 5% temples(+30%). Total growth rate bonuses will be 80%. It would go as follows: 

10,000 * (1 + (0.03 * (1 + 80/100))) = 10,240

The net change is +240 peasants and your new total population will now be 10,240. The new total population will obviously be limited by your max population.

Homes or Barracks?

This question gets asked at least once every round in your council and it's an understandable one since it's pretty tricky. But here are some tips.

Homes house 30 people whereas Barracks house 36 military units. This means that for housing military, Barracks are 20% more efficient than Homes: 36/30=1.2. However, Barracks do not house peasants, which Homes do. A very common thing to do then is to achieve 20% population bonus, at which point Homes are equal to Barracks (30*1.2=36).

Certain races are dependent on a landtype for their defense and they all have their Homes on that landtype. These races should always build Homes and aim to get +20% population bonus as quickly as possible, except for perhaps Gnomes (see below).

Then we have races with a negative population bonus, namely Wood Elves (-10%), Gnomes (-5%) and Troll (-5%). In the Wood Elf's case, running Barracks is not an option since they rely on forest for defense and offense and the Wood Elf's units are so efficient on their own that you don't really need the full +20% population bonus. Gnomes can be played in two ways: fast with Barracks and slow with Homes. Indeed, Gnomes rely on a landtype for their defensive units, but they only need 40% Mountains and their units cost a lot of ore (see above) so they can use Barracks instead of Homes, since Ore Mines will take up a large portion of the 40% mountains. A combination of both Homes and Barracks is sometimes used. Trolls can go either, but Barracks are typically preferred due to the speed they give (in that you don't have to waste time and resources on getting +25% population bonus).

Bonuses

There are many types of population bonuses that can be used to increase your entire Population. They are:

  • Castle Improvements (Keep)
  • Racial bonus
  • Wonder (Fountain of Youth and School of War only)
  • Tech (Urban Planner and Urban Mastery only)
  • Prestige (this is the only bonus that is multiplied independently of the other bonuses)

Population bonus is most often achieved by investing resources in Keep (see Castle Improvements). Some races have a positive population bonus, which should also be accounted for. Goblins and Kobolds start with +20% and +40% respectively, excluding initial prestige, so they should never build Barracks. However, it is not rare to see these with improved Keep anyway, since they need a lot of units and every unit is one peasant "lost".

The School of War only affects the amount of military units that are held by Barracks only. Obtaining the School of War means that Barracks will hold an extra 15% troops giving each Barracks the capacity to hold 41.4 military units in Base Ruleset and Imp Ruleset, and 42.55 military units in the Tech Ruleset.

Prestige also counts towards your population bonus, but unlike all other population bonuses acts multiplicatively, by increasing the bonus itself: (1+Prestige Bonus)*(1+Other Bonuses). The Prestige Bonus has a multiplier of 2.0 for any Ruleset (see Prestige). An example from the Base Ruleset is:

  • You have 500 prestige bonus (so 500/250*2 or 4%), 10% population bonus, 2% keep bonus and 5% wonder bonus, your Total Population Bonus is in fact: 
1.04 * 1.17 = 1.2168 or 21.68%

An important note in the Homes vs Barracks debate is even if you build Barracks, you are still going to need to invest in Keep since all buildings (except Homes and Barracks) house 15 people and are affected by population bonuses. For example, 25% Homes with a 6.67% Population Bonus will house the same as 25% Barracks with no Population Bonus. Another example, an 8% bonus with no homes will house the same as 10% Barracks with no bonus. For these reasons, most players rely on homes and population bonuses and only run barracks during the first two weeks.
