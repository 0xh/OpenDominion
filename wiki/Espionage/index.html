Espionage

From The Dominion Encyclopedia

Jump to: navigation, search

Espionage and Spy Ops are an important part of the game. Espionage is used in order to gain Information on targets, steal resources from other Dominions or as Black Ops in order to harm opponents and threats. Whether you succeed or not is based on your SPA (Spies Per Acre, also called Spy Ratio), spy strength, the target's SPA, the target's spy strength, a certain random factor and a difficulty factor. The random and difficulty factors (variables) are unknown but all forms of stealing are equally difficult.

The Ops

Here is a list of all the spy ops available in the game.

Information Gathering

Although spies do not cost any resources like Spells do, if you fail an operation, some of your spies are likely to be captured and lost. The number lost is dependent on your SPA compared to your target's SPA. Depending on ruleset, these operations cost 2% (Base and Imp) or 3% (Tech) Spy Strength.

Operation Effect Notes
Barracks Spy (BS) Gives an overview of the target's army and see units home defending (turtling), units out on attack and units in training. Barracks Spies are vital to attacking. They can also be used to replace Clear Sight if the target has impenetrable wizards. It is important to know that all numbers except units in training are only 85% accurate (15% inaccurate) and that every BS will (is extremely likely to) produce unique numbers every time.
  • Units at home must be divided by 0.85 to get the highest possible value from the lowest known value.
    • Example: After several Barrack Spies, the lowest amount of units acquired is 423. The highest possible value is 423/0.85=498 (round up).
  • Units returning from battle are to be multiplied by 0.85 to get the lowest possible value from the highest known value.
    • Example: After several Barrack Spies, the highest amount of units returning home is 3313. The lowest possible value is 3313*0.85=2815 (round down).

Note that calculating returning units is only useful if you also have a Clear Sight with which to compare. In the above example (if we assume the target is a Merfolk) with 498 units home and 2815 returning home, without a Clear Sight, the lowest DP from Krakens is 498*2=996 raw defensive power. However, if you get a Clear Sight and see the target has 3133 Krakens, we can now compare units returning home to the turtle. The Clear Sight shows the target has 3133 Krakens and since we know he has at least 2815 Krakens out, his turtle cannot exceed (3133-2815)*2=636 raw defensive power.

Castle Spy (Ca, CS) Reveals the target's Castle Improvements. This will show you what the targets Improvement page looks like, except wonder (if any) is not shown. For that, there is Vision. Masonries are already included in the bonus shown, so there is no need to calculate them manually.
Survey Dominion (SD) Reveals the target's buildings. Surveys also reveal incoming buildings and barren land. Surveys do not show of what landtypes the barren land consists. SDs are crucial in attacking as they will show what GTs and FHs the target has. It is also important when checking Dark Elf and Nox, as those two races depend on a specific building and buildings on a specific landtype respectively.
Land Spy (LS) Reveals the target's landtypes. This is only useful when checking Gnome, Icekin, Sylvan and Wood Elf. However, in most cases a Survey will suffice as it shows buildings and incoming building, whence amounts of specific landtypes can be calculated. The only time Land Spy is necessary is if buildings of the races' specific land type shown in a Survey do not reach or exceed the maximum for the races' units and there is barren land. Wood Elves do, however, not have a maximum making a Land Spy necessary at all times when there is barren land.

Resource Theft

You can steal resources from other Dominions, but only if they are larger than you and within your range (and you within theirs). All resources can be stolen, but the amount you can steal has certain limitations. These operations cost 5% Spy Strength.

Operation Effect Notes
Steal Platinum Steals platinum from the target.
  • Platinum theft is limited to 2% (per grab) of the target's platinum.
  • Platinum theft is also limited to 75% of your own raw platinum production.
  • Each spy can at most carry 45 platinum.
  • In order to count towards Spy Success and the Platinum Thievery rankings, 5000 platinum per grab is required.
  • Note that Forest Havens may reduce the amount of stealable platinum.
Steal Lumber Steals lumber from the target.
  • Lumber theft is limited to 5% (per grab) of the target's lumber.
  • Lumber theft is also limited to 75% of your own lumber production.
  • Each spy can at most carry 50 lumber.
  • In order to count towards Spy Success and the Lumber Thievery rankings, 2000 lumber per grab is required.
Steal Mana Steals mana from the target.
  • Mana theft is limited to 3% (per grab) of the target's mana.
  • Mana theft is also limited to 56% of your own mana production.
  • Each spy can at most carry 50 mana.
  • In order to count towards Spy Success and the Mana Thievery rankings, 1000 mana per grab is required.
Steal Food Steals food from the target.
  • Food theft is limited to 2% (per grab) of the target's food.
  • In order to count towards Spy Success and the Food Thievery rankings, 2000 food per grab is required.
  • Other limits unknown.
Steal Ore Steals ore from the target.
  • Ore theft is limited to 5% (per grab) of the target's ore.
  • Ore theft is also limited to 68% of your own ore production.
  • Each spy can at most carry 50 ore.
  • In order to count towards Spy Success and the Ore Thievery rankings, 2000 ore per grab is required.
Steal Gems Steals gems from the target.
  • Gem theft is limited to 2% (per grab) of the target's gems.
  • Each spy can at most carry 50 gems.
  • In order to count towards Spy Success and the Ore Thievery rankings, 500 gems per grab is required.
  • Other limits unknown.

Non-War Black Op

Although spies do not cost any resources like Spells do, if you fail an operation, some of your spies are likely to be captured and lost. The number lost is dependent on your SPA compared to your target's SPA. This number is twice as high as spies lost on Information Gathering operations. There is currently only one espionage black-op that does not require War. These operations cost 5% Spy Strength.

Operation Effect Notes
Assassinate Draftees Kills some of the target's draftees.
  • The amount of draftees killed depends on yours and the target's Spy Ratios.
  • In order to count towards Spy Success and the Military Assassin rankings, 50 draftees per grab must be killed.
  • In case of mutual war, 50 draftees must be killed in order to gain prestige.

War-Only Black Ops

These black-ops are only available during war and can be performed as soon as war has been declared. In case of mutual war, prestige may be awarded for successful operations. These operations cost 5% Spy Strength.

Operation Effect Notes
Assassinate Wizards Kills some of the target's wizards. This operation kills wizards but not ArchMages, Dark Elf Adepts or Sylvan Dryads. The amount of wizards killed depends on the involved parties' SPA and the target's wizards.
Magic Snare Reduces the Wizard Strength of the target. This operation is rarely considered useful. It is harmless in small doses and requires several players dedicated to one target to have an impact. Its use is primarily to decrease someone's Wizard Strength to a point where the target cannot cast defensive spell. On average, 2-3% Wizard Strength per grab is lost.
Sabotage Boats Sinks some of the target's boats. The amount of boats sunk depends on how many boats are protected by docks, the involved parties' Spy Ratio and how many boats the target has. It is uncommon for one dominion to be able to sink another dominion to a point where the target cannot send out his forces anymore, but this depends on how protected and prepared the target is. On average, 2-3% unprotected boats per grab are sunk. It is common practice to cast Great Flood on sunk dominions to slow their boat regrowth.

Spy Strength

Spy strength regenerates with a constant rate of 4% every hour. With the Spy Master tech, spy strength will regenerate at 5% every hour. Finally, with the Dark Artistry tech, spy strength will regenerate at an almost doubled rate of 7%.

Spy Success/Prestige

When performing espionage black-ops or theft and having great success, the game will award you Spy Success (also called Spy Prestige) which is not related to regular prestige, nor should it be confused with the prestige one gains from successful black-ops during mutual war. It serves no purpose outside of the Most Successful Spies rankings.
