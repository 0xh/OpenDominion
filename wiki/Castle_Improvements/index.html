Castle Improvements

From The Dominion Encyclopedia

Jump to: navigation, search

Castle Improvements are a vital part of any efficient strategy. They are not as important (if used at all) when playing a speed-dependent strategy. They are used extensively by explorers and converters, while attackers use them to a lesser degree.

Investment

Castle parts are improved by the player investing resources into a specific part. Different resources are worth different amounts of improvement points; platinum is worth 1 point, ore and lumber are worth 2 points and gems are worth 12 points. Platinum is rarely used for improvements, as it can be used to build buildings and train units. Lumber is only used for improvements by Wood Elves, Sylvans and Orcs (although the latter rarely and should not focus on this). Not all races need ore to train their units and they should never build ore mines. Ever. The only use for gems is to improve the castle (although late-round, gems may be better of better use exchanged into platinum). This makes Diamond Mines the best building if the player intends to invest heavily in castle.

Racial differences

It is arguable whether Icekin and Gnome benefit more from having Diamond Mines or Ore Mines. The most common however is that Icekins run excessive Ore Mines (up to and above 1000), but some are seen having a few hundred DMs (rarely above 200). Gnomes vary more, but DMs are common. Wood Elves and Sylvans cannot fit DMs into their build since they need a high amount of forest. Nox and Dark Elf can run DMs without any problem, despite having units that depend on a landtype and/or building.

Other races are more relaxed and can vary more.

The castle parts

There are six parts of the castle into which the player can invest resources.

  • Science
    • Bonus: Increases platinum production.
    • Comment: Used extensively by explorers but also by attackers that focus a lot of improvements. A common heuristic is to go 1% Science for every 5% Keep.
  • Keep:
    • Bonus: Increases maximum population.
    • Comment: Crucial and used by all races and play styles.
  • Towers:
    • Bonus: Increases wizard strength and magic resistance.
    • Comment: This adds a bonus to your WPA (Wizards Per Acre), making every wizard and Arch Mage (Towers)% stronger. Also, Towers decrease the amount of damage taken from war-only black ops such as Fireball, Lightning Bolt and Disband Spies spells (but doesn't affect the effectiveness of other black ops).
  • Forges:
    • Bonus: Increases offensive strength.
    • Comment: Especially useful for races with inefficient OP, but all attackers and converters are seen with Forges.
  • Walls:
    • Bonus: Increases defensive strength.
    • Comment: After Keep, the second most used improvement. Used by all races and play styles.
  • Harbor/Irrigation:
    • In base/imp:
      • Bonus: Increases food production, boat production and protection.
      • Comment: Rarely used in large amounts, irrigation is often underrated. It is fairly easy to get it to 20%, which in turn will lower the amount of farms needed, thus saving up land. In recent times, bonuses to boat production and protection were added due to the increasing impossibility to protect all of one's boats towards the end of the round.
    • In tech:
      • Bonus: Increases food production.

Formulas

Under all rulesets, the castle improvements formula is: 

MAX*(1-e^(-1*POINTS/(4000*LAND+15000)))

...where MAX is replaced by the maximum for the improvement, POINTS is the amount of points the player has invested (see above) and LAND is the landsize.

However, during the Base Ruleset, the formula is changed for Walls and Forges in order to make it harder to achieve high numbers. The regular constant 4000 is increased to 7500, making the formula: 

30*(1-e^(-1*POINTS/(7500*LAND+15000)))

The formula for Towers and Irrigation/Harbor is also changed in the Base Ruleset to reduce the speed at which higher numbers can be achieved. The regular constant is increased to 5000, making the formula: 

40*(1-e^(-1*POINTS/(5000*LAND+15000)))
