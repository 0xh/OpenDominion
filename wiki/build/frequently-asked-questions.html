General gameplay

How do I play this game?

Read The Complete Newbie Guide.

Attacking and Defending

What DP (or DPA) do I need?

You need to have more DP than OP of the strongest attacker in your range. Having more than that is stupid and having less is why you get hit. To do this, see the attack guide but instead of calculating the DP, calculate the OP. Be sure to include incoming units and remember that the person may not be able to send everything since he too has to defend his lands.

  • See top OP.
  • See also the DP chart, but read the disclaimer and heed the warning.

Why was my army fought back? Why did I bounce/fail an attack?

Because the OP you sent was not higher than your target's DP. You have to send at least 1 more OP than the target's DP. Sending OP equal to the target's DP will result in a failed attack. See the attack guide.

Why did I fail an attack against someone who had no army at all?

Things that are too good to be true typically are too good to be true. All dominions have a minimum defense of 

Landsize * 1.5 * Modifiers

Which means that a dominion that is 250 acres with no buildings, walls, spells and no army at all (including no draftees) will have 

250 * 1.5 * 1 = 275

275 DP.

Heroes

Why is my hero not gaining levels?

In order for your hero to level up, you have to send him on targets 75% your landsize or greater. Heroes are however still awarded victories for successful attacks below 75%. Victories and levels are not the same.

I have two (or more) heroes with the same name, what do I do?

There is nothing you can do about this. Thankfully, however, you will see hero name, type and level on the invasion page making it easy to send the right one. If you have two (or more) heroes with the same name and of the same type, base your decision on what level they have.

Do hero bonuses stack?

If you have two (or more) heroes of the same type, only the bonus from the one of highest level will be counted. If they are all on the same level, still only one of them will be counted.

Military

Are ArchMages worthwhile?

If you are playing fast attacking, chances are AMs will not be worthwhile for you because they cost quite a bit in the long run and you will also lose wizards for the 12 hours when AMs are in training. If you are playing a medium or slow paced strategy and thus (typically) have a greater economy AMs will pay for themselves in that they will not die and you gain power of two wizards at the cost of one peasant.

What Wizard Ratio should I have?

An attacker rarely needs more than 0.4-0.7 WPA. That is normally enough to get ops on possible targets.

Sylvans and Dark Elves have for natural reasons exceptionally high WPAs. Icekins that are attacking will need 5 WPA to obtain maximum offensive power from their Ice Elementals.

What Spy Ratio should I have?

An attacker rarely needs more than 0.5-0.75 SPA. That is normally enough to get ops on possible targets. Explorers that cannot be active enough to avoid having their plat stolen should have high SPAs, whereas active explorers only need enough to keep track of top-OP in their range.

Lizardfolks and Halflings have for natural reasons exceptionally high SPAs.

Buildings

How many Diamond Mines should I build?

This depends on strategy and race. Good Wood Elves and Sylvans are never seen building DMs (the exception being Wood Elves playing an LBM-strategy). Icekin and Gnome may build DMs but may choose not to and instead focus wholly on Ore Mines. Nox and Dark Elf, despite depending on certain buildings, build DMs.

Nox, Dark Elf and all other races will build amounts of DMs that suit the strategy and also the ruleset. Fast attackers build few (200-300) DMs and only do so to maintain a miniscule castle, often focusing almost entirely on Keep in order to boost their population. Slower players build up to 500-600 DMs. Explorers and converters may sometimes build up to 1000 DMs. As mentioned, ruleset is also a factor. During the Base Ruleset improvements are quite good and DMs are seen in normal amounts. The Tech Ruleset is known for its rather weak improvements (mainly due to DMs producing merely 14 instead of 15 gems per hour; but also because of infavourable maximums making the investement-formula slower). As its name hints, the Imp Ruleset is when improvements are at their finest and DMs are thus particularly useful and common.

Should I build Forest Havens?

No. There is no known situation or scenario where building Forest Havens is a better option than building something else, even if you are Wood Elf or Sylvan. It is a mystery why the building remains in the game.

Race specific

Orc

Is the racial spell worthwhile?

Yes, it very much is, despite the increased casualties.

Mathematically, one could argue that there are no increased casualties. For example, if we assume that you need 1000 Bone Breakers without casting Bloodrage to break a target, you will lose 

1000 * 0.085 = 85

85 Bone Breakers. If you however cast Bloodrage, you can leave 10% of the 1000 Bone Breakers at home and you will thus suffer 

900 * 0.085 * 1.1 = 84.15

84.15 casualties, 1% fewer than if you had sent 1000 Bone Breakers without Bloodrage.

This does not mean that Bloodrage reduces casualties by 1%, unless you always train 10% more OP than you need. It means that it is not worthwhile to train 10% more OP merely to avoid the increased casualties.

Spirit/Undead

Why are they so weak? Why are they not strengthened?

Contrary to popular belief, Spirit/Undead are very strong races but they are also among the most difficult in the game. They require constant activity and not a single wrong step the whole way. Inexperienced players do not play them correctly (building the wrong things and training the wrong units all at the wrong times). For this reason, Spirit/Undead are not recommended to newbies. The two races are not in any acute need of strengthening.

Troll

Brutes?

If you are attacking, then you will need Brutes early on to get started. Eventually, you will outgrow them and instead train solely Smashers.

Ogres?

No more than 2000.

Bashers?

No.

Smashers?

Yes. And lots of them.
