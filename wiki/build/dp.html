Defensive power

DP, short for Defensive Power, refers to how strong your army is on defense. All military units have a value for defensive power and offensive power (although it may be 0). In the scribes, under Military, you can see what values your race's units have.

Raw & Mod DP

Raw DP is the total Defensive Power of the troops that you're sending without including any bonuses that would affect it (such as Guard Towers, Walls and defensive spell).

Modified DP (also mod/modded DP, final DP) is your raw DP with all bonuses included.

Example

If you are a Human and have to 3,000 Knights and 500 Archers, your raw DP will be: 

3000*6+500*3 = 19500

With that in mind, let's assume you have 8% Walls, 5% Guard Towers, Imperial Armada, Ares' Call and that it's Base Ruleset: 

(3000*6+500*3)*(1+(8+5*1.75+5+10)/100) = 19500*1.3175 = 25691.25

Temples

An attacker with Temples will reduce the defensive modifiers of the dominion he is hitting. If we take the above example and put against a dominion with 5% Temples, we get: 

Temples: 5*1.75=8.75% reduction
DP after Temples: 19500*(1.3175-0.0875) = 19500*1.23 = 23985

There is never a maximum for Temples other than that it may never reduce a target's defensive modifers to less than 0. Keep in mind that a race's units' values, as well as the multiplier for Gryphon Nests, may vary depending on ruleset and that rulesets are switched every round.

Turtle

Turtle refers to defensive power of offensive units. For instance, under Base Ruleset the Human Cavalry has 3 turtle DP, the Troll Smasher has 7 turtle DP and the Dark Elven Spirit Warrior has 0.

Artificial turtle

Races that lack turtle defense for their elite attackers are often seen training so called artificial turtle. In the case of Dark Elves, this is accomplished by training more Adepts than necessary for defense and sending the extra Adepts out together with Spirit Warriors. This comes somewhat more natural to Lycanthropes, whose elite unit Garous convert enemy units to Werewolves which in turn can be used for artificial turtle.

Attacking with artificial turtle is not the same as suiciding.

Bonuses

All bonuses to defensive power are additive, meaning that they stack and are not multiplicative. For example, 10% DP from a wonder, 5% from Walls and 10% from a spell adds up to a bonus of +25% DP. 

10 + 5 + 10 = 25
