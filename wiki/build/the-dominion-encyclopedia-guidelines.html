Content

  • Only submit material which is relevant to Dominion.
  • Search first. Do not create duplicate articles.
  • Do not create new articles where your new text instead can be added to a currently existing article.
  • If there already is an article about the same (or similar) subject and you can use that article as a template design for yours, do so. See Races.
  • No Liege Lord articles. Only mention Liege Lord where absolutely necessary, such as the Monarchy article and Alliances.

Spelling and Grammar

  • Spell check your text and check the grammar closely. It does not matter whether you use British/Commonwealth English or American English.
  • Make sure that your text keeps an encyclopedic tone and that it is easy to read. Remember, a complete n00b might read and they should be able to understand without too much problem.
  • Do not use k to shorten thousands. For example, write the full 100,000 and not 100k.
  • Use platinum instead of plat.
  • It is recommended that the first sentence of the article begins with bold text until the titleword of the article is reached. If the first word is the titleword, only it is to be bold. Example: 
The game defines activity as the amount of time since your last log in...

Formatting

  • No more than one consecutive whitespaces (no double-spacing).
  • Only one empty line between paragraphs (no double linebreaks).

Stories

  • Stories not written specifically for The Dominion Encyclopedia are not subjects to these guidelines and shall not be altered in any way except one:
    • Linebreaks that, due to the way the MediaWiki software parses them, do not appear as they should, are to be altered by adding one
      for every missing linebreak.

Mathematics

  • Check your calculations before submitting.
  • Use ^ as exponent. Example: 
5^5=3125

Rules

There is to be absolutely no:

  • Spam
  • Graffiti
  • Abuse
  • Flaming/laming
  • Offensive material

Player biographies

  1. Notability. The player should be someone that is known in the community for his actions in-game.
  2. Privacy. Personal information (such as age, real name, and picture) should only be added if the player has provided these details before.
  3. Neutrality. Keep an encyclopedic tone and objective stance; whether it be a friend or foe you write about.
