Atteching

From The Dominion Encyclopedia

Jump to: navigation, search

Atteching (rarely called techtacking) is the act of attacking while also teching. This has been a discouraged play style for quite some time, until the dawn of the Tech Ruleset, although it has been done before that. Some rounds after the first Tech Round, a new feature was implemented into all rulesets where attackers would gain Research Points (RPs) by attacking. By this time, the term atteching was coined. An attecher utilizes both the "conquered RPs" together with Schools. See RP production.

It is especially common to attech in Tech Rounds, but it is also successfully done in other rulesets.

Race Selection

All races that are good for attacking can attech, although the fast races should avoid taking more techs than absolutely necessary to not slow themselves more than what they can recover.

However, during Tech Rounds, the following races benefit especially from atteching:

  • Halfling
  • Kobold
  • Nox
  • Troll

Humans also benefit from teching but since they need such a vast amount of techs, atteching is not a recommended play style. It is arguable if the same applies to Dark Elf. They do not need as many techs as Humans, but they have no turtle and are generally quite limited in their build since Adepts rely on Wizard Guilds.

Building Selection

An attecher has almost the same build as an attacker, but sacrifices something (typically homes/barracks or mods) to fit Schools. The most important decision is how many schools to build. For someone wanting only three techs (most commonly Military Genius), building 50 schools either during protection or quickly OOP will be enough to have the tech within roughly two weeks. For those seeking 7 techs, 100 to 200 schools are recommended. Higher than that and you have to ask yourself if what you are doing will pay off in the long run or if you are better off exploring and teching.

Unit Selection

Essentially the same as attackers. An attecher may need to focus more on elite units since the schools may take up space otherwise used for homes/barracks. If the attecher is sacrificing mods for schools, it will be necessary to train more offensive or defensive units than normally.

General information

Due to RPs gained on attacking, many players will get at least one tech per round without building schools. This is, however, not called atteching, but is rather considered a small bonus for attackers.

Pros

  • Once you have the techs, you have a bonus over everyone else and you have still managed to keep up
  • Prestige

Cons

  • Difficult
  • Risky

Recommendations

  • Intermediate attackers wanting to try something new
