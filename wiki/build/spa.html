SPA and SR are short for Spies Per Acre and Spy Ratio. 

Spies / Landsize = SPA = SR

What SPA should I have?

The Spies Per Acre ratio (found on the Statistics Advisor's Page) is the main determiner in whether a dominion will be easy to spy on or whether many will fail espionage against that dominion. It also determines whether they will be able to successfully spy on other dominions. The SPA of the two dominions involved are compared, and the dominion with a higher SPA will be able to get ops on dominions that have lower SPAs. There is a small chance built into the game where dominions with much higher SPA will fail operations on dominions with much lower SPA, and vice versa where the dominion with lower SPA succeeds against a dominion with a much higher SPA. Generally, dominions with a similar SPA to their target will have about a 50% chance of successful spy operations on them. Spy Ratio's over 10 effectively count as 10.

An attacker rarely needs more than 0.5-0.75 SPA. That is normally enough to get ops on possible targets. Explorers that cannot be active enough to avoid having their plat stolen should have high SPAs, whereas active explorers only need enough to keep track of top-OP in their range.

Lizardfolks and Halflings have for natural reasons exceptionally high SPAs.
