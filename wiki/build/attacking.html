Attacking

From The Dominion Encyclopedia
Jump to: navigation, search

Attacking is considered by many to be the only true way of playing. An attacker gains land only by invading other Dominions and conquering their lands for themselves. This is done by training both offensive and defensive units and by sending enough OP to overcome a target's DP. Attacking has yielded the most round winners by far and most experienced and skilled players will opt to attack full round as their style of play. Attacking does not have to be done right from the start. Many players choose to convert after exploring for part of the round.

Race Selection

All races can be attacked with. There are however, certain races that it is easier to convert with rather than attacking full round. These are generally more efficient races that would be too slow or expensive to attack full round:

  • Gnome
  • Wood Elf
  • Icekin
  • Troll (if planning to go all elites)

See Converting for more details.

Building Selection

This depends on race and how the player wishes to play the race. Certain races require certain buildings although almost all strategies and races will need to have maximum Smithies if they wish to attack (Gnomes and Wood Elves may be exceptions). Other than full Smithies, building selection is up to the individual strategy. It is recommended that players aim to maintain a high population through keeping many Homes or Barracks. Mod buildings may also be utilised for increasing military strength although unless you are running a fast strategy, generally the longer you can go without using mods, the better your long-term prospects are.

Unlike Explorers, attackers need high numbers of Towers for Wizard ops and Docks for transportation of troops (with some Race exceptions). Attackers will therefore need to dedicate more of the land to vitally important buildings than Explorers will although the rest of their land is more freely available to strat variation than it would be for an Explorer.

Unit Selection

Unit selection is perhaps the most key element of determining how an Attacker will perform during a round. Previously, it had always been recommended to maintain almost purely elite units with minimal spec units. Recently however, fast attacking has become more prevalent with the smaller playerbase and there is more freedom to use specs more widely. During the Imp ruleset, high use of specs is a very key part of strategy early on with almost all of the early leaders making use of high numbers of specs instead of the more expensive elites.

In order to find out and calculate a target's DP, Spy and Wizard operations are required to determine military numbers and Mods of their defensive power. Spies and Wizards are therefore needed in addition to OP and DP for Attackers in order to complete these vital ops. Most Attackers will maintain 0.5-1.0 SPA and 0.4-0.7 WPA (unless a Spy or Wiz Race).

General information

Attackers are what make Dominion interesting. It is through fear of being invaded that you must build DP and react to what large Attackers are doing within the game in order to manage an effective finish. Attacking is not only considered the true way to play but it is also seen as being the most fun way as well. No matter what your goal is during a round, whether it be a high finish or having the most victories, Attacking can be an enjoyable way to play for most.

Pros

  • Fun
  • Vast majority of Round Winners have been all-round Attackers
  • More strategy and risk involved than Exploring

Cons

  • Can be very difficult to pick up
  • Finishes are generally less consistent than Exploring

Recommendations

  • Anyone
