Prestige

From The Dominion Encyclopedia
Jump to: navigation, search

Prestige is a bonus that increases a dominion's population, attack strength, and food production. For every 100 prestige, a dominion will receive a 1% increase in each of the aforementioned resources. Conversely, a dominion can also receive proportional penalties if it has negative prestige.

All dominions begin with 250 prestige. The highest prestige one can have is equal to one's landsize when the land from the most recent hit comes in, meaning that if one is 250 acres large, has 250 prestige and conquers 30 acres and 32 prestige, the new prestige will be 280. However, the prestige gained in battle is actually brought home by the slowest troops you send, so if you have a 9 houring race, then you will need to send some (12-houring) specs if you are at risk of capping your prestige. This crucial mistake often occurs when a 9 houring race such as Goblin decides to play a 333-acre strategy.

Gaining Prestige

There are 3 ways to gain prestige:

  • Attacking targets between 75% and 120% one's own size will gain prestige based on the target's prestige. This is capped at 10% of one's own prestige plus 20. This is the most common and effective method of gaining prestige.
  • Black ops against targets with mutual war declarations. This will net a small amount of prestige provided that sufficient damage is done. Thievery operations do not gain prestige.
  • When a wonder is rebuilt after being destroyed/taken from another realm, all dominions in the realm gain 100 prestige. The realm from which the wonder was taken does not lose any prestige. But if the realm already owned a wonder, no prestige will be gained.

Formula

The formula for gaining prestige is based on the target's prestige. Thus, hitting someone with a lot of prestige will give you more prestige. 

(0.05*[Target's prestige])+20

Losing Prestige

There are several ways to lose prestige:

  • Invading a target that has recently been hit multiple times (this may instead result in smaller prestige gains, depending on how any times the target has been hit).
    • 1st-3rd hits: normal
    • 4th hits onwards: negative. You lose more the more times the target has been hit.
      • Note 1: There is actually a time span given between the hits, that resets the hit count after a certain time (believed to be between 12-24 hours).
      • Note 2: Bounces do not count into those hits. For example, if a dominion got hit thrice but one of which is a bounce, hitting it the 4th time still yields positive prestige.
      • Note 3: If your realm owns the Golden Throne, for the 4th and subsequent hits, the wonder in fact doubles the prestige penalty.
  • Failing an attack by 15% or more (Raze).
  • Being successfully invaded (5% prestige loss).
  • Receiving black ops during a mutual war, usually in small amounts (1-3 prestige). The amount of prestige loss by the target will then be "stolen" by the black opper.
  • Hitting dominions that are less than 2/3 of your landsize

Prestige Bonuses

There are several methods of increasing the amount of prestige gained:

  • War. Prestige gains increase by 15% with one-way war relations, and by 25% with mutual war relations.
  • The Golden Throne gives all dominions in the realm a 50% increase in prestige gains through invasion.
  • In the tech ruleset, shrines increase prestige gains by up to 40%. Note that this bonus does not apply to the constant 20 prestige and only to the 5% prestige you take from the target. 
Assuming max shrines and your target has 250 prestige.
250 * 0.05 = 12.5 ≈ 12

Raw prestige gain: 12 + 20 = 32
Mod prestige gain: 12 * 1.4 + 20 = 36.8 ≈ 37

In this scenario, the actual prestige bonus is a mere 15.625%. You have to hit someone with approximately 350,000,000 prestige to get the full 40%.
