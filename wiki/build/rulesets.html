The Rulesets

Ruleset is a term used to describe the varying sets of rules used in Dominion. There are currently three of them, colloquially called Base Ruleset, Imp Ruleset and Tech Ruleset. A new ruleset is set every round, with the Base Ruleset being used between every round with the Imp Ruleset or Tech Ruleset. Simply put: Base, Tech, Base, Imp, Base, Tech and so forth.

Base Ruleset

This ruleset is called "Base Ruleset" because it is the basic one. Up until round 35, this was the only ruleset, that was changed a bit between rounds. It is usually played once in every 2 rounds.

Rounds with this ruleset since the introduction of rulesets: 37, 38, 40, 42, 44, 46, 48, 50, 52, 54, 56, 58, 60, 62, 64, 66, 68, 70, 72 and 74.

Tech Ruleset

Late round 35, Ross announced that he had some major changes coming up in order to "shake things up." (See Round 36 changes.) The presented changes showed a lot of units getting entirely new bonuses based on techs, as well as other changes. It was hence dubbed the "Tech Ruleset" and was the second one. Castle improvements are especially weak under the Tech Ruleset, using a less beneficial formula and DMs producing only 14 instead of 15 gems (93.33% the regular production).

Rounds with this ruleset: 36, 39, 43, 47, 51, 55, 61, 65, 69 and 73.

Imp Ruleset

The Imp Ruleset, which is short for (Castle) Improvements Ruleset, is called so because castle improvements are exceptionally good, while mod buildings are less prominent. Good and evil are mixed, meaning that realms are not alignment-specific and good and evil races coexist in realms. Smithies reach max bonus at merely 15% and the cost of elite units went up by approximately 10% compared to the Base Ruleset. This was the third ruleset. A maximum of 10 players in a realm was in effect during Round 49 and Round 53 but this has been reverted back to the original 15 recently.

Rounds with this ruleset: 41, 45, 49, 53, 57, 59, 63, 67 and 71.

Differences

The differences between the rulesets may be unclear by just reading the scribes, so here is a presentation of the many differences and changes.
