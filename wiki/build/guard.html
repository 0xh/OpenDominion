Players may join different Guards in order to protect themselves and constrain their range. Range here refers to a certain upper and lower percentage of your own landsize. A certain guarded range means that people within that range are the only ones that can take action against you (attack, steal from, spy on, cast spells and so on). There are other special ranges that do not themselves offer any protection.

The guards

To join a guard, click Government and scroll down to Guard Membership, where there is a button called "Join the Royal Guard". If you click that button and for 24 hours do not take action against anyone outside of the Royal Guard range, you will be a member. Once you are a member of the Royal Guard, you may join the Elite Guard and if you for 24 horus do not take action against anyone outside of the Elite Guard range, you will be a member.

Once you have joined a guard, you cannot leave (drop guard) for 48 hours. To attack a wonder you must not be in guard, so make sure to not join a the guard within 48 hours of taking a wonder.

No guard

  • Range
    • Minimum: 40% (landsize*0.4)
    • Maximum: 250% (landsize/0.4)
  • Comments
    • This is the full range when not in any guard at all.

Royal Guard

  • Range
    • Minimum: 60% (landsize*0.6)
    • Maximum: 166% (landsize/0.6)
  • Comments
    • You will lose 2% of your platinum production as payment to join and be in Royal Guard.
    • Used by mid-range explorers and some bottomfeeding attackers, this range offers no real extra security. Some argue that it keeps them safe from bottomfeeders, while most bottomfeeders attack within the Royal Guard range. Late-round, many attackers leave Elite Guard to attack for easy land within Royal Guard.

Elite Guard

  • Range
    • Minimum: 75% (landsize*0.75)
    • Maximum: 133% (landsize/0.75)
  • Comments
    • Must first be in Royal Guard in order to join Elite Guard.
    • The -2% platinum production remains (it neither increases nor vanishes).
    • +25% cost of exploration.
    • Used exclusively by attackers who do not want to leave themselves open to bottomfeeders or much smaller dominions that steal resources or perform spy ops or spells on them. There are rare cases of extremely unskilled explorers who join the Elite Guard, despite the +25% increase of exploration cost.

Special ranges

Dark Elf Spirit Warriors are not considered to have a "special range" even if their OP varies depending on target's landsize. The reason for this is that 75% is the range when they reach their most commonly full potential and become immortal. Attacking <75% with Spirit Warriors is not considered good conduct and sane playing. There are rulesets when Spirit Warriors gain +0.5 OP if attacking 95% or higher, but this is rarely ever done and is not a viable strategy.

Gnomish attack range

  • Range
    • Minimum: 85% (landsize*0.85)
    • Maximum: none, may depend on guard status
  • Comments
    • Gnomes will rarely attack targets smaller than 85% their range, since their elite OP units do not reach full power against targets below this landsize. Attacking below 85% with Gnome makes the Juggernaut rather inefficient.

Prestige range

  • Range
    • Minimum: 75% (landsize*0.75)
    • Maximum: 120% (landsize*1.2)
  • Comments
    • This range isn't a very important one for other races than Orc, whose elite DP depends on prestige.

Spirit & Undead conversion ranges

Elite conversions range

  • Range
    • Minimum: 65% (landsize*0.65), techrounds: 50% (landsize*0.5)
    • Maximum: none
  • Comments
    • This is the range Spirits and Undeads ideally should keep hitting in all the time. Attacking in this range makes elite OP units convert to elite DP, which is the most efficient form of conversion.

Specialist conversions range

  • Range
    • Minimum: 50% (landsize*0.5), techrounds: does not exist
    • Maximum: none
  • Comments
    • When a Spirit or Undead attacks targets >50%<65%, their elite OP units will convert some enemy casualties into specialists. Attacking in this range should be avoided if possible, since SPUD specialist DP is cheap enough to train and specialist OP is just terrible.
