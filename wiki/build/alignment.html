This is a legacy article. The contents is out of date and kept solely for historical and reference purposes.
Reason: As of round 67, no alignment bonuses remain in the game. The article is marked as legacy due to the high likelihood of alignment bonuses vanishing completely.

 

 

 


Races are divided according to alignment, separating good races from evil races. This is of decreasing importance, save for Tech ruleset rounds when good and evil races are pitched against each other more than in other rounds by racial bonuses and units that gain more OP against the opposite alignment. Evil and good races may be placed in the same realms, a change first introduced in the Imp ruleset and carried over to the other rulesets as of round 64.

The Nox is the most prominent alignment-based attacker, with The Lich units gaining +1 OP against good. Humans and Nomads have a spell than gives them +10% OP against the opposite alignment and also lets them kill Undeads (if Human) and Spirits (if Nomad) on offense.

There were once neutral races, which could join realms of either alignment, but this feature has been taken away and the affected races are now reassigned with either alignment or abandoned.
