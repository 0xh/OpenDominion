Black ops

From The Dominion Encyclopedia

Jump to: navigation, search

Black ops (also blackops and black-ops) are spy and wizard operations that damage opposing dominions. As well as being split between espionage and spells, black ops can be further split into two categories: war-only ops and regular. Black ops are most effective when a number of players from the same realm join forces to target one dominion in particular. By combining their efforts, there is little a dominion can do to defend themselves, especially if the black ops are done by surprise, such as declaring war on an unsuspecting realm. Black ops are a great way for explorers to make a difference in a round, and also earn prestige in case of mutual war. Recently, Ross has decided that black ops will not be enabled until the 8th day of the round.

Black ops are usually performed on strong dominions that pose a threat or have a weakness that can be exploited to gain advantage. The damage done from black ops can cripple even a leading dominion so it is vital that leading players are at least aware of the threat and make preparations to defend.

Defending

The easiest way to defend against black ops is to have a good Spy Ratio and Wizard Ratio so that those attempting black ops will fail. It is important to note that black ops are harder to successfully accomplish than information ops so you will not need to necessarily match your opponents' ratios but the higher your ratios, the more likely they are to fail. Casting Energy Mirror also greatly affects the opponents as their Black Ops will be reflected upon themselves.
