Casualties

From The Dominion Encyclopedia

Jump to: navigation, search

Your units are subjects to casualties as described below.

Offensive

Offensive casualties are 8.5% of the units needed to break the target, regardless of how many you send. This means that if you send 10,000 Lizardmen (60,000 OP) to break only 54,000 DP, you will suffer 9001*0.085=765 casualties and not 10000*0.085=850, since only 9001 Lizardmen are required to break the target.

Against wonders, all units (including immortal units and units with reduced casualties) suffer 5% casualties.

The only exception to these two rules are Gnomish Suicide Squads which always suffer 50% casualties. Never more and never less. Not even against Onyx Mausoleum.

Defensive

Base defensive casualties, believed to be around 6.6%, are also based on the relative landsize of your target; if you hit a 95% target, you will give them more casualties than you would give a 75% target. Oversending will also increase defensive casualties. Defensive casualties decrease for each consecutive hit within the last 24 hours:

  • 1st hit: 100% of base casualties
  • 2nd hit: 80% of base casualties
  • 3rd hit: 60% of base casualties
  • 4th hit: 55% of base casualties
  • 5th hit: 45% of base casualties
  • 6th hit: 35% of base casualties

Casualty Reductions

There are a number of different types of casualty modifiers which are split into the two following categories: unit bonuses and non-unit bonuses. An example of a unit bonus would be Firewalker's Salamander having -50% casualties, and the Troll and Orc racial spells. The second category is comprised of all other modifiers, such as the Healer hero, Shrines, techs and wonders. When determining your total casualty bonus, all non-unit bonuses are added together and capped at a -80% bonus, then the remaining casualties are multiplied by the unit bonus of the units being sent into battle. Here is an example of a Firewalker with 9% Shrines and the Tactical Battle tech during a Normal round. 

[0.09 * (-6.5) - 0.25] = -83.5% casualties; This is then capped at -80%.
1 - 0.8 = 0.2 * 0.5 (Salamander reduction: [1 - Racial Bonus]) = 0.1

Therefore, this Firewalker's Salamanders will take 10% of the normal 8.5% casualties; in other words, 0.85% of the Salamanders needed to break the target will die.
