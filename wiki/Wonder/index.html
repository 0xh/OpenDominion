To take a wonder

Nowadays, wonders are taken by destroying them after which they are automatically rebuilt in your realm. All wonders start in realm #0, appearing at a random time (always 12 PM, 6 PM, 12 AM or 6 AM game time) throughout the round. All wonders in #0 have 250,000 hitpoints and the first realm to attack it down to 0 gets it. Several realms may be attacking the same wonder in #0 and since you cannot get a clairvoyance on #0 there is no sure way of knowing about this (unless you do CVs on all realms).

Once a realm has captured a wonder from #0, it is given a new amount of hit points. For wonders taken from #0, the following usually applies: 25000*(Days-1) where Days should be replaced by the day of the round the wonder is taken. For wonders taken from another realm there are two maximum HP formulas. The Daily HP cap is as follows: 42500*(Days-1). If a wonder taken from another realm has an HP less than half of the current daily HP cap, the following is applied: Current HP*2. The hitpoints-formula for wonders hit by multiple realms is currently unknown.

  • If a wonder is destroyed by another realm that holds a wonder at the same time, the wonder will be brought back to realm 0. The amount of hit points that the wonder will then hold is unknown, however, for a fact it will not be 250,000 hit points.

Wonders can be taken from other realms too. To do this, War must be declared on the realm in question. Exactly 24 hours later, your realm can start sending troops on the wonder by clicking Invade, picking the realm and scroll down to the wonder-attack view. When attacking wonders, your troops will always suffer 5% casualties (except Gnome Suicide Squads which always suffer 50% casualties); this includes immortal units.

In ages past, repairing wonders was possible, but this was taken away since it made it nearly impossible for realms to take another realm's wonder.

Special-OP units

Some units may seem confusing when attacking wonders. Units that depend on a landtype or building have the OP they have with the landtype or building included. Icekin Ice Elemental has the OP it has based on your Wizard Ratio. In tech rounds the Lich, Cavalry and Spirit Warrior have their tech-dependent +1 OP bonus.

  • Dark Elf Spirit Warrior: 5.5 OP
  • Dwarf Warrior (techrounds): 7 OP
  • Gnome Juggernaut: 5 OP
  • Lycanthrope Garous (techrounds): 7 OP


Wonders of the World

Ancient Library & Halls of Knowledge

  • Base
    • +10% value to Castle investments
  • Imp
    • unchanged
  • Tech
    • +20% value to Castle investments
    • +10% research point gain
  • Comment
    • Ancient Library and Halls of Knowledge are two identical wonders. The effect may be a bit confusing, but here's how it works: for as long as you have this wonder, every 1 point you invest in castle improvements will be worth 1.1 and this increased effect will remain even if you lose the wonder. But if you lose the wonder, every 1 point goes back to being worth solely 1 point.


City of Gold

  • Base
    • +10% platinum production
  • Imp
    • unchanged
  • Tech
    • +7.5% platinum production
  • Comment
    • A very good, solid wonder that is often underestimated.


Factory of Legends

  • Base
    • Reduces building construction cost by 25%.
  • Imp
    • Reduces building construction cost by 35%.
  • Tech
    • Reduces building construction and rezone costs by 25%.
  • Comment
    • Good for explorers and bottomfeeders, especially so under the Imp Ruleset. Attackers benefit very little from this wonder as they should attack at least 75% their size anyway and thus get -50% construction cost. More useful to them during the tech ruleset, with the removal of several wonders and the rezone cost addition.


Fountain of Youth

  • Base
    • +5% max population
  • Imp
    • unchanged
  • Tech
    • unchanged
  • Comment
    • This wonder will increase your population by 5% additively. You will often see top realms have this wonder early and then lose it to get a more high-profile wonder.


Gnomish Mining Machine

  • Base
    • +30% ore production
  • Imp
    • unchanged
  • Tech
    • unchanged
  • Comment
    • A very good wonder for Icekins and Gnomes and rather helpful for races with relatively high ore costs such as Troll and Human. Do not take this wonder just to aid your ore production a little.


Golden Throne

  • Base
    • Invasion prestige gains for controlling realm increase 50%.
  • Imp
    • unchanged
  • Tech
    • unchanged
  • Comment
    • When prestige growth is at its fastest (early in the round), it's too soon to take a wonder, which is why Golden Throne hardly ever is taken by the top realms. It is still a good wonder if you are attacking often. On particularly good hits, this wonder can give you 30 or more prestige.


Great Market

  • Base
    • +10% employment and +5% platinum production
  • Imp
    • unchanged
  • Tech
    • unchanged
  • Comment
    • This wonder is particularly good for explorers or attackers with very high population bonuses such as kobolds and goblins. The Employment bonus has a few errors and shows the employment figures incorrectly on both the Status page and the Statistics Advisor page, although the actual effect of the bonus is correct.


Great Oracle

  • Base
    • +30% wizard power, mana costs reduced by 15%
  • Imp
    • unchanged
  • Tech
    • unchanged
  • Comment
    • As with Guild of Shadows but for wizards, this will increase your Wizard Ratio by *1.3. Mana costs will go down by 15%, costing *0.85 of their normal cost. Not a popular nor demanding wonder.


Guild of Shadows

  • Base
    • +50% spy strength
  • Imp
    • +75% spy strength
  • Tech
    • +25% spy strength
  • Comment
    • Similar to Great Oracle. Getting spy-ops on a realm with this wonder can be pretty painful but it is not impossible, so do not use this wonder to try to hide your poor DP. Your total spy strength is calculated by your Spy Ratio and multiplying by the spy strength bonus. Example, using the Base Ruleset, if you have a SPA of 0.75 and with this wonder, you will have 0.75*1.5=1.125 modified SPA.


Hanging Gardens

  • Base
    • +50% food production
  • Imp
    • +75% food production
  • Tech
    • +60% food production
  • Comment
    • Good, but nothing too fancy.


High Cleric's Tower

  • Base
    • Everyone in Realm can kill Undead and Spirits
    • Dark Elf Spirit Warriors attacking owner's Realm die like other units
  • Imp
    • unchanged
  • Tech
    • unchanged
  • Comment
    • This wonder kills Undead and Spirit units on both offense and defense. Undeads, Spirits and Lycanthropes that have this wonder can attack other Undeads and Spirits and gain converts.


Imperial Armada

  • Base
    • Boats in a realm with this wonder cannot be sabotaged (sunk)
  • Imp
    • unchanged
  • Tech
    • No platinum production penalty for Dominions in the Royal or Elite Guard, and boats cannot be sabotaged in this Realm.
  • Comment
    • A very handy wonder for those who worry about being sunk. This used to have a defensive bonus attached which has now been removed. However in the Tech Ruleset, dominions with this wonder recover the -2% platinum production loss if they are under Guard.


Ivory Tower

  • Base
    • 50% chance of causing spells cast against owner's realm to fail.
  • Imp
    • unchanged
  • Tech
    • unchanged
  • Comment
    • Useful if your realm is in a black-op war but it does not hide your poor DP. Annoying if you have low WPA and doing wizard ops on dominions with this wonder.


Onyx Mausoleum

  • Base
    • Attacks against dominions in the realm controlling this wonder suffer 25% more casualties than normal.
  • Imp
    • unchanged
  • Tech
    • Attacks against dominions in the realm controlling this wonder suffer 10% more casualties than normal.
  • Comment
    • Do you think a mere +25/+10% casualties are going to keep me from attacking you? Think again. The acres gained will compensate the extra casualties.
    • This wonder does affect immortal units but only if they send less than 85% of their target's DP. If they do that, it will hurt. A lot.


Portals of Transportation

  • Base
    • Troops (and land) return 3 hours faster from battle
  • Imp
    • unchanged
  • Tech
    • unchanged
  • Comment
    • What is not mentioned in the scribes is that you also get your land back 3 hours faster and not just troops, which generally is argued as being the one big flaw with this wonder. Your units will only be away for 9 hours and it is a fun wonder if you are playing on intermediate level. Units that already return in 9 hours will return in 6 hours.


Ruby Monolith

  • Base
    • Battle casualties reduced by 20%
  • Imp
    • Battle casualties reduced by 30%
  • Tech
    • Battle casualties reduced by 30%
  • Comment
    • Not worthwhile except in Imp Rounds.


School of War

  • Base
    • Barracks house 15% more troops.
  • Imp
    • unchanged
  • Tech
    • unchanged
  • Comment
    • There is often a bit of confusion here. School of War makes barracks house 15% (percent) more troops (not +15 troops) which makes barracks house 37*1.15 = 42.55 troops (not 36+15 = 51). If many people in a realm play with barracks, this wonder will be very useful.


Spire of Illusion

  • Base
    • Clear Sights on the controlling realm's dominions are now only 85% accurate.
  • Imp
    • unchanged
  • Tech
    • unchanged
  • Comment
    • This wonder is probably one of the leading causes of unsuccessful invasions. Clear Sights on a realm with this wonder should be treated like Barrack Spies.


Tomb of Kings

  • Base
    • Double prestige gains against monarchs
    • +5% platinum production
  • Imp
    • unchanged
  • Tech
    • unchanged
  • Comment
    • The platinum bonus is the most usable bonus this wonder has to offer. Rarely seen in the top realms.


Underground Society

  • Base
    • 50% chance of causing spy operations against owner's realm to fail.
  • Imp
    • unchanged
  • Tech
    • unchanged
  • Comment
    • Useful if your realm is in a black-op war but it does not hide your low DP. Annoying if you have low SPA and doing spy ops on dominions with this wonder.


Wizard Academy

  • Base
    • Reduces spell damage done to controlling realm by 50%.
  • Imp
    • Reduces spell damage done to controlling realm by 75%.
  • Tech
    • Reduces spell damage done to controlling realm by 50%.
  • Comment
    • This is often considered the worst wonder in the game. Its only use would be in the case of a black-op war, but hardly even then.


Retired Wonders

These wonders have been removed and no longer in the game.

Apocalypse Stone

  • Base
    • Dominions in the realm controlling this wonder suffer no effects from fear.


Great Wall

  • Base
    • +10% defensive power
  • Imp
    • +5% defensive power
  • Tech
    • unchanged
  • Comment
    • This wonder was arguably one of the best. If you lost it, you lost 10% DP, which is worth considering. However, you would not lose a wonder out of the blue, so you would have time to prepare.

Abandoned as of Round 55.


Lair of the Dragon

  • Base
    • +10% offensive strength
    • +5% defensive strength
    • -20% food production
  • Imp
    • +10% offensive strength
    • +5% defensive strength
    • -30% food production
  • Comment
    • Prior to removal, this was one of the most powerful and popular wonders in the game.

Abandoned as of Round 55.


Monument of Protection

  • Base
    • +5% defensive strength
  • Imp
    • unchanged
  • Tech
    • unchanged
  • Comment

Abandoned as of Round 55.


Obelisk of Power

  • Base
    • +5% offensive strength
    • +5% defensive strength
  • Imp
    • unchanged
  • Comment
    • Prior to removal, this was one of the most powerful and popular wonders in the game.

Abandoned as of Round 55.


Planar Gates

  • Base
    • Increases offensive strength against Planewalkers by 20%.
    • Increases defensive strength against Planewalkers by 10%.

Abandoned as of Round 23.


Temple of Equality

  • Base
    • Produces the effect of 5% temples
  • Imp
    • Produces the effects of 3% temples.
  • Comment
    • What this wonder does is place 5% hypothetical temples in your dominion. It mainly benefits attackers but explorers will find it slightly useful as it increases population growth rate. The overall strength of this wonder depends on how strong temples are in the given ruleset, making it the strongest when temples have a high modifier.

Abandoned as of Round 55.


Temple of the Blessed

  • Base
    • +10% offensive power for GOOD races only
  • Imp
    • +5% offensive power for GOOD races only
  • Tech
    • Does not exist
  • Comment
    • Under Base ruleset, this is one of the wonders with highest priority. It is not considered as precious under the Imp ruleset. Not only because its power is halved, but also because alignments are mixed and as such it may not benefit enough people in a realm or pack.

Abandoned as of Round 55.


Temple of the Damned

  • Base
    • +10% offensive power for EVIL races only
  • Imp
    • +5% offensive power for EVIL races only
  • Tech
    • Does not exist
  • Comment
    • Under Base ruleset, this is one of the wonders with highest priority. It is not considered as precious under the Imp ruleset. Not only because its power is halved, but also because alignments are mixed and as such it may not benefit enough people in a realm or pack.

Abandoned as of Round 55.
