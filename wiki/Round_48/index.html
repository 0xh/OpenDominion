So, as an explorer there isn't much of a story to my round. So I'll just be stating the main points that helped me get there.

First of all, congrats to IC, Tjock, Anders and Nedo. You all played rounds deservant of the win.

My round win started 4 months ago, when myself and my housemate MeggaMortY thought of running an all specs explorer with max mods. The stategy was to work on the principle that huge landsize relative to days into the round (or required DP), would allow for the very large military required, while keeping an even larger number of peasants.

We ran some numbers for each of the races with potential - Gobbo, Kobbo and Lyc produced the best results obviously, and Kobbo was the best out of the three, as the whole strat was about pop bonus. Their growth rate was also attractive as there was a great fear of being FB'd. So, Kobbo was decided as the race for my round win. I ran numbers that suggested, due to the strat we were playing, that I would be able to run a LOT of alch's while maintaining max employment. I considered masonries, but some calculations and general knowledge showed that they weren't as good as alchs. NOTE that in the end we went for masons because Quendi also ran some numbers and he found mason would be better than alchs, and he had only 600DM’s – we had 800. And Quendi would know… We also decided to get 3 techs, to -10% exploring.

As we decided to do this during round 45, and I was going to be busy working for 46 and 47, round 48 was going to be the first chance to do it. And I guess it was the best round type, as there is no bump of exploring cost. As MeggaMortY was playing round 47, I got him to record top op's and top land every day.

When I finished my mining engineering work, I returned home and commenced a full round Excel simulation (that's right, around 1250 days of simming - in a 28MB Excel file). I simmed optimally for the first 5 or so days, and then changed to exploring and constructing every 12 hours. I thought this would produce a worse result than what I would actually be doing during the round, as draftees were allowed to build up in the sim. However I didn't account for not being about to store plat for longer than really 1 hour. For DP, I used the top op's for every day of round 47 as a guildeline, and trained underlings to match it. This was the major error of the sim, as I didn't really think about having to defend 4:3's, although I always thought in the back of my head that I'll probably need to. I just didnt think about how much difference it would make.

So, I simmed to day 31 and then stopped, because the results already showed I was going to own the round. In the simulation on day 31, I had 6850 land and 450k income. I believe the only reason this didn't materialise is that the simulation defence was around 25% less than what I ran during the round, and that accounted for a lot less income and exploring early on in the round, which worked exponentially as the round progressed.

The strategising, calculations and simulations before the round started was where the round was really won, we just had to put them into practise.

I followed my sim perfectly for the first 5 days, didn't miss an hour. At this point we noticed a 3rd big kobold in our realm, who looked very similar to us. I messaged him and asked what his strategy was, and it looks like it's similar to mine. He replied: "I'll be surprised if anyone is doing something similar to me." A week later when we were still looking the same, he told me his strat and it turns out (as assumed) that it was exactly the same, and that it was Quendi. Yep, there's some coincidence for you.

So the 3 of us continued to get ahead. Quendi, with his superior exploring skills, was managing to stay slightly ahead of us and our exploring tech. However at whatever point in the round it was, I started to overtake him. I think it was coincidental that he decided to delete at this time, because he was bored. Well played Quendi.

We defended top 4:3's from like day 5 onwards. I really dont know if that's what explorers normally do, but it seemed very necessary this round, as there were so many suicides, and we were ahead by a LOT. I'm quite sure that if someone could hit us, they would have. So Top OP may have been low, but 125% of Top OP was high.. Particularly because it was Kobolds with the big OP, and therefore huge 4:3 power. If we hadn't run so much DP we would have finished 10k+, I have no doubt. So it was a GOOD strat thankyou very much.

We also ran around 1 wpa throught the round, for fear of bein FB'd. That didn't happen (kinda unfortunately).

AT some point in the round I was WAY ahead of everyone (over 20% ahead of anyone), and they were all held down by Turb. #5 were down to 30k peasants and less - they were really hurting and I was sure that I had won the round comfortably. But then, Turb decided to farm out to them, giving 2 of them 1200 acres in a couple days. So they were thrown right back into the race, with my land gap reduced severely, and their peasants pumped up to 70k+ from 30k. I don't know if he actually believes he didn't make much difference farming out, but he sort of really did.

Around day 40 I was sitting on 480k income or so, so decided to stop building and train some elites before getting back into lings for the home straight, allowing peasants to drop.

When Anders (I Brontosaurus) blew ahead and held huge Top OP and even huger 4:3, we were just waiting for some realm to war and black-OP him, as he was holding EVERYBODY down. 20 was most likely to do so, so we were CVing often, hoping to see it. When they did go for it, we joined in, which may have been a big mistake. We kinda sunk anders, but that's about all our 1.4 spa could manage, for some stupid reason. And then, as we were really only watching the Kob's 4:3, I missed Tjocks immense OP, until I caught him CS me. I then calced him in the next 5 minutes, and then Visioned to see Military Genius, and realised he could break me by a few K. I then hit refresh to see himself and Nedo assassinating hard (but failing a lot). I panicked and released all my 2k wizards and 3k spies asap, then refreshed again to see him bounce.
