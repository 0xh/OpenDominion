The buildings

There are many different buildings and it can be hard to decide which one to build.

Production buildings

  • Alchemy - Produces platinum, but should rarely be built at all after protection.
  • Diamond Mine - Produces gems which are the most common resource used for castle improvements. This building should not be measured in percentage, but rather in a fixed number. You will rarely see attackers have more than 600 DMs. Depending on race and ruleset 300-500 DMs the optimal amount.
  • Dock - Produces boats which most units require to be sent out on attack. Also protects boats from being sunk. For small players 20 docks overall usually is enough, for people in the top 5-8% docks is a must.
  • Farm - Produces food. The amount of food produced varies a bit depending on ruleset but a race without a food bonus or penalty typically needs 8% farms.
  • Factories - Reduces construction and rezone costs. They are useful in protection but are rarely seen after that. Some explorers may keep a number of these to reduce construction costs, but it's usually better and more efficient to use the land for something else.
  • Lumberyard - Produces lumber which is one of the two resources used for constructing. Wood Elves and Sylvans are seen with enormous amounts of these since they need them to keep up their forest percentage and use lumber for castle improvements, but other races will hardly ever need more than 2.5%.
  • Ore Mine - Produces ore. Gnomes need ridiculous amounts of these (at least 15-20%). Icekins are often seen with lots of Ore Mines too since they need high mountains to keep their DP up, using ore for castle improvements. Other than that, do not build more than you need. 1-3% is enough for most races. Trolls and Humans (attackers) may need 3-5%.
  • School - Produces research points which are used for teching. Plan extremely carefully ahead before you build these. Normal percentage range from 20-30% but do not build these unless you know what you are doing.
  • Tower - Produces mana which is used for spells. The amount you need depends greatly on how you play. 4% is enough for an explorer who wants to have enough mana to cast self spells, Fool's Gold once a day and do Clear Sights on strong attackers to calculate their OP. For attackers, 5-8% is enough. If you plan on using your race's spell a lot, you will need up to 8%. People who focus on offensive spells can be seen having over 10%.

Mod buildings

If you want to play an efficient strategy, mod buildings should typically be used very sparsely until late late-round when efficiency no longer is as important as speed.

  • Gryphon Nest - Increases your modified offense. Used mainly for runaway attempts and races with inefficient OP troops.
  • Guard Tower - Increases your modified defense. Used mainly for runaway attempts and races with inefficient DP troops.
  • Masonry - Increases your castle improvements. Used mainly by explorers. Only useful in combination with 750+ Diamond mines.
  • Shrine - Reduces your offensive casualties and, during tech rounds, increases Prestige gains. Only useful if you attack 24/7 and/or have 9h return troops. Not recommended unless you know what you're doing.
  • Temple - Reduces your target's modified defense. Increases your population growth rate. There is no point in having too many of these, especially during Tech ruleset when their multiplier is low compared to Guard Towers and Gryphon Nests. Build during protection to quickly get peasants.

Miscellaneous buildings

  • Forest Haven - Gives peasants defense. Reduces spies lost during an operation, fireball damage, and platinum stolen. Even with all these bonuses, it is still not worth building to help your overall dominion.
  • Smithy - Decreases cost of units. This building is among the most important. As an attacker, you should always have full (max) Smithies. The maximum varies on ruleset so be sure to check that. Since Smithies do not reduce the ore cost for Gnomes, a Gnome should never build Smithies.
  • Wizard Guild - Increases Wizard Strength rate. Decreases costs of Wizards and Archmages as well as spell costs. If you are a Dark Elf, 23% WGs are considered the ideal amount.

Homes and Barracks

  • Homes - houses 30 peasants; affected by population mods.
  • Barracks - houses 36 (37 in Tech Ruleset) military units; unaffected by population mods.

See population for details.

Construction & Rezone

Construction comes at a cost of platinum and lumber with rezoning only requiring platinum. The formulas used to determine build and rezone costs are shown below. Factories are applied to the final cost. 

Platinum cost
850+([Total Land]-250)*1.53

Lumber cost
88+([Total Land]-250)*0.35

If you have under 1250 constructed buildings, the formulas are slightly different. In place of [Total Land] in the above formulas, instead use either [Constructed Buildings] or [Total Land * 0.75], whichever is higher. 

Rezone
250+([Total Land]-250)*0.6

Bonuses

All construction and rezoning bonuses are multiplicative with each other. Firewalker, Sylvan and Wood Elf have, under some rulesets, racial bonuses for construction or rezoning costs. Assuming a Firewalker with Factories, a builder hero, Conquerors Crafts and Factory of Legends, the final cost would be: 

(Original/raw cost) * (Racial bonus) * (Factories bonus) * (Hero bonus) * (Tech bonus) * (Wonder bonus)
