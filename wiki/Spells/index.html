Spells are a vital part of Dominion. Spells cost mana which is produced by towers.

Self Spells

Self spells are spells that affect your own Dominion and cannot be cast on others. The number in the cost column is the spell's mana Cost multiplier, which is multiplied by your landsize. For instance, Gaia's Watch costs 2*Landsize mana.

Spell Cost Effect Notes
Gaia's Watch 2x Increases food production by 10%. Before casting this spell it is recommended to determine if the increased mana upkeep costs more land in terms of percent towers is higher or lower than building more farms.
Ares' Call 2.5x Increases defensive power by 10%. Used by all races except Halfling, Icekin and to some extent Kobold. Useless for attackers whose offensive units are home if they have turtle since that alone should keep them safe.
Midas Touch 2.5x Increases platinum production by 10%. The most important self-spell and should be on at all times.
Mining Strength 2x Increases ore production by 10%. The most useful spell for Gnome & Icekin, and to a small extent Human/Nomad, Troll, Nox and Goblin. Most races that require ore are better off building a few more OMs, only casting Mining Strength in case of emergency.
Harmony 2.5x Increases population growth rate by 50% (the speed at which new peasants are born). This spell is only useful in three situations: during protection, if being fireballed or if enormous amounts of land have been obtained.
Surreal Perception 4x While active, if you are on the receiving end of non-information-gathering spells or spy-ops (such as Clear Sight, Barracks Spy etc), the performer's dominion name will be revealed to you. With its atrocious mana cost, this spell should only be used if you absolutely have to know the name of the dominion who is responsible. Otherwise it is a waste of mana.
Energy Mirror 3x For the duration of this spell, all successful spell cast unto you have a 20% chance or being reflected back at the caster. A life-saver in case of severe black ops, its accuracy is questioned, often cited as reflecting more than 20% although this has been repeatedly refuted by the powers that be. Do not keep this running all the time since the mana cost is obnoxious in the long run together with other spells. In recent rounds, reflected spells now appear in your Recent News.
Fool's Gold 5x When cast, your platinum is protected from theft for 10 hours; can only be cast once every 22 hours. Cast this if you have a high income, will be inactive for a few hours and fear people will steal from you. Less useful for Halflings & Lizardfolks due to their natively powerful spies.

Offensive Spells

Offensive spells are, as the name indicate, spells that you cast on other dominions to either get information from them (information ops) or to hurt them (black ops). The number in the Cost column is the spell's mana cost multiplier.

Information Op Spells

Spell Cost Effect Notes
Clear Sight 0.5x Gives you an overview of the target dominion in the form of their Status screen. The most important and useful offensive information-gathering spell, this will give you a copy of the target dominion's status screen (minus spies, wizards and Arch Mages). Useful for target finding, top-OP scouting, finding someone to steal from etc. If the realm has the wonder Spire of Illusion, Clear Sights will be treated like Barrack Spies.
Vision 0.5x Reveals the target dominion's techs and heroes, if any. As the description suggests. Has become increasingly useful due to the rise of atteching. Also use to track top OP to see if they have attack bonuses (Military Genius & Magical Weaponry).
Revelation 1.2x Shows spells currently affecting the target dominion. Generally used to see if a target is inactive and has no defensive spell on (usually Ares Call). It is also used to see if someone is suffering from any offensive spells like Insect Swarm, Great Flood etc.
Clairvoyance 1.2x Reveals the target's realm's Town Crier. Useful for target finding, tracking top OP and taking wonders when it is imperative to know if the other realm is at war with other realms.
Disclosure 1.2x If the target dominion's realm owns a wonder, its HP will be disclosed. Paramount when taking wonders. Serves no other purpose.

Non-War Black Op Spells

The duration of these spells will stack up to at most 12 hours, which means that if you cast a spell once and the duration is 8 hours you can cast it once more (most likely for another 8 hours) and it will stack up to 12 hours.

Spell Cost Effect Notes
Plague 2.7x Slows population growth rate by 50% (the invert of Harmony). On its own a rather harmless spell, but if cast on a dominion that is suffering from multiple fireballs, it can be devastating.
Insect Swarm 3x Slows food production by 15%. Can be very problematic if cast on dominions with low food. Races with food penalties (increased food consumption or decreased production) suffer especially from this.
Great Flood 3x Slows boat production by 50%. As with Plague, this spell is harmless on its own. It is normally used together with sinking during Black Ops to keep the target sunk. It can be detrimental on dominions with low amounts of docks as they are constantly flooded.
Earthquake 3x Slows ore production by 10%. While it may bother all races that need ore, it is really only useful against Gnomes.

War-Only Black Op Spells

These spells are designated War-Only, because they can only be cast during a war. The sole exception to this is if a dominion has been invaded in the last 24 hours. During those 24 hours, the invaded dominion (whether the invasion was a success or bounce) can cast war-only spells unto the invader or invaders. These spells stack just like non-war black-ops.

Spell Cost Effect Notes
Fireball 3.3x Kills peasants and destroys food. Often considered the most deadly of all magic black-ops due to its relatively low cost and high firepower. Peasants and food are incinerated proportional to how much of both are left. Fireball rankings go by the number of peasants killed.
Lightning Bolt 4x Damages Castle by destroying resouces invested. Only useful against targets with high castle improvements as the high mana cost makes failed attempts painstaking. Science and Irrigation/Harbour are not affected by Lightning Bolts.
Disband Spies 5x Turns some of the target dominion's spies into draftees. Rarely worthwhile, but it can sometimes be used in combination with sinking or snaring. Does not affect Halfling Master Thieves or Lizardfolk Chameleons. Expensive spell.

Racial Spells

Almost all races have their own spells. Some races share spell. The multiplier for most racial spells is 5x. The only exception is Parasitic Hunger. Click the box next to Race to sort by race.

Spell Race Effect Notes
Alchemist Flame Firewalker Increases alchemy production by 15 platinum (10 platinum in Tech Ruleset). Good for as long as a large portion of your land consists of alchemies. Later on, homes and smithies will be more worthwhile for attackers while explorers may keep running lots of alchemies and thus make the spell worthwhile.
Blizzard Icekin Increases defensive power by 15% (not cumulative with Ares' Call). The Icekin pride, this spell is well worth the extra mana compared to Ares' Call.
Bloodrage Orc Increases offensive power and casualties by 10%. Despite the casualty increase, this spell is very much worthwhile. See FAQ.
Crusade Human Gives 5% attack strength bonus and allows you to kill Undead. Very good spell and a nuisance to Undeads.
Defensive Frenzy Halfling Increases defensive power by 20% (not cumulative with Ares' Call). This spell is both a blessing and a curse. While it gives an obscene amount of extra defense, Halflings are very vulnerable if they let the spell run out. Keep it on at all times, except if you are attacking and have enough turtle to be safe without it. The spell is most commonly referred to as Frenzy and that is what it says on the Magic page in the game.
Erosion Lizardfolk and Merfolk 20% of captured land automatically re-zoned into water. Often called the least useful of all racial spells, due to its high cost, low bonus and that the two races rarely build high amounts of homes until late in the round.
Gaia's Blessing Wood Elf Gives +20% food production (not cumulative with Gaia's Watch) and +10% lumber production. An excellent spell. It is definitely worth keeping on at all times, or at least as often as possible.
Howling Kobold Increases defensive and offensive power by 10% (not cumulative with Ares' Call). One of the finest spells in the game, it should always be used when attacking. However, if +10% defense is all you need, it's better to go for Ares' Call.
Killing Rage Goblin Gives +10 offensive power. Very good. Very useful. Use when attacking. If 9-hour attacking, one casting should be enough for a set of two hits.
Mechanical Genius Gnome Base/Imp Ruleset: -30% re-zoning costs. Tech Ruleset: +10% offensive power if your army is composed of 75% or more Juggernauts. Late-round in Base/Imp Rounds, the spell can save a lot of platinum. In Tech Rounds, it serves as +10% OP spell unless you train more than 25% Suicide Squads, making it very useful.
Miner's Sight Dwarf Gives +20% ore production (not cumulative with Mining Strength). A good spell that is a little expensive early on, but later on work perfectly with Miners.
Nightfall The Nox Increases offensive power by 5%. Very good.
Parasitic Hunger Undead and Lycanthrope Increases base converts from 6% to 9%. See conversion. The only racial spell to cost 5.5x.
Regeneration Troll Reduces casualties (both offensive and defensive) by 25%. While not useful early on for an attacking Troll (due to attacking with Brutes which are cheap units), this spell will save you millions of platinum and hundred-thousands of ore late-round when attacking with Smashers. If an exploring Troll knows he is going to get hit, casting Regeneration will dramatically decrease the amount of units lost.
Unholy Ghost Dark Elf Enemy draftees to not participate in battle. Sounds better than it is, although it is definitely good. For OOP attacking the spell can be a serious drawback if you attack dominions that rely heavily on draftees for defense, because the defense of the target will be too low so its landsize is reduced by the game before you attack (see also scripting. It can be good and handy throughout the round though, especially if attacking inactive explorers.
Warsong Sylvan Gives +10% offensive power. Excellent spell. As with Goblin's Killing Rage: if 9-hour attacking, one casting should be enough for a set of two hits.

Wizard Strength

Wizard strength regenerates with a constant rate of 4% every hour. With the Master of Magi tech, wizard strength will regenerate at 5% every hour. Finally, with the Dark Artistry tech, wizard/spy strength will regenerate at an almost doubled rate of 7% per hour.

Wizard Success/Prestige

When casting offensive spells and having great success, the game will award you Wizard Success (also called Wizard Prestige) which is not related to regular prestige, nor should it be confused with the prestige one gains from successful black-ops during mutual war. It serves no purpose outside of the Most Successful Wizards rankings.
