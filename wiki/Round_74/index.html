General Information

Registration opened on the 8th April 2012, the round commenced on 13th April 2012, and ended on the 2st of June 2012.

Round Win Story written by Sauce and Round Loss Story written by Oment.

Round Win Story

Get yourself a beverage, get comfortable and allow me to tell you the tale of #16 and #29, a tale woven with triumph, heartache, honor and deceit. This is the Round 74 Recap.


Pre-Round:

Our pack this was essentially the same as last round with the addition of Petra in place of Lorian.

Faith went human, playing his version of my 8k DM heavy Human from last imp round, but adapting the name of his Sylvan that rd (Battle Rain)

I played a teched converter WE (Velveeta Village)

Takao wanted WE but since I had ‘called it’ first he went for an oop attacking gobbo converter (Back in time)

Kuki was LDE sylvan (The real McCoy)

Petra’s plan was to imitate Lorian’s masonry LDA strat from Rd66 (Mercredi)

Vance went for a teched gnome converter with big imps, a similar strategy to Petra’s without the slow start. I think he had the best strat of any of us, but got hit too many times.


When the round began, we realized that we had another four-pack in the realm, so we knew we had potential for a big round.


Boss played a converting human (Once Upon a Time)

Round Loss Story

Well, that’s not the result I was expecting to be writing this to. Firstly, congratulations to Faith. Well played. The rest of you who think they deserve kudos… Just read the story.


For the record, our DE was Lightning, who played with Erik/bond/fingal/ladeda (and some others) previously on the wins in R64 and 67. I know half of Dom expected it to be Erik and I heard some people were assuming it was ladeda, but it wasn’t.


There were no changes in our pack from the previous round, so strategy talk started pretty early. Lightning really liked DE and went garg/SW with them (Russian roulette). Erik took Halfer, hoping to emulate his R70 successes (Chicken Pox). Both of them would be going fast out of the gate. I took FW with a general gameplan of going slower than the others with an idea of going for more elite D by the time I hit 10k spec D (slow.internet.guy). Imperion took Gobbo, hitting once OOP and once at W6+14, then explore and reconvert later (The Invadinator). Bond took Troll (Mucho Badasstico) and Daniel took WE (WoodOfSteal). We planned to war once or twice to get rid of competition, so the explorers would be FBing early on and snares were on the menu for day 7.


Protection was boring, couple of screw-ups in pack notwithstanding. We had another Troll in there, who turned out to be tetre in the end. Day 3 revealed likely top threats in 9, who looked to be doing at least four fast attackers, possibly more. They also warred #14 on day 3, which absolutely demolished #14 and gave 9 a fair boost. Other threats weren’t really clear, but given who was in #14, it’s likely that they would’ve been a good threat.


OOP went fair for all of us, in a low-key way. Lightning gained 163a on a Sylvan, Erik had a lousy 100a and I ended up with 126a. Daniel also managed to somehow change his sim so that he ended up as top Dom on 806a or so. A suicider in 28 – I heard it was Sylar? - made things interesting. He hit #9 Viserion, who was a 700a huge alch FW. That pretty much took him out right from the word go as his economy never really recovered. Despite him gone, 9 was basically the prime threat at first. Their Halfer and DE were right on top and their Liz and Kobbo not too far behind. War target was set on 9 pretty soon, with snare going to DE and FB later decided.


We didn’t have a lot of top OP early. 9 had all of it past day 5, with their Halfer going huge mods. The DE had been playing with huge drafts for most of day 6, but we still managed to semi-miss his dump of 800 Swords, coinciding with lighty’s return on day 7. It turns out it wasn’t so much of a planned kill, but it was still dangerous. War was on, and in no-time we snared the DE and unleashed fireballs on the Halfer, whose pez took a minor dive to 7k from 10-11k.


Unfortunately, 9 also had two explorers with decent spies which meant I was also snared, albeit less than their DE. The DE ended up hitting Lighty at the start of the hour, and Erik sacrificed himself to hit the DE in return. He ended up getting tripled by 9, but it took the DE down. Lightning also did a small Swordies push of his own and ended up hitting Meraxes in 9, who was another threat and out on Erik just a wee bit light. DE was still top OP, but without turtle and without Ares, he was easy pickings for Erik again when war became active. He immediately (14 seconds) retaliated on lighty, but I managed to follow on the DE, while being snared and without Ares.

I should have died there. I was three hours away from casting Ares; four after a snare just after HC did some more, but I wasn’t snared at all after that, and that was it. DE was pretty much dead, Kobbo was crippled, my Dom was back up after stagnating for 40h and Lightning had also managed to get 9 to take mostly barren, so all was good there.


The Swords push also meant that next to nobody could hit into our realm safely without copping a hit themselves.


Unfortunately, some people have too much DP for their own good, which meant that #23 was able to make that safe hit. This effectively ended Erik’s attacking and he decided to unconvert and reconvert at a later stage.


Day 9 was when #14 also warred 9, presumably for revenge, and along with #28 chipping in, just about everyone in 9 copped FBs. The Halfer and Liz ended up making FHs which countered the FBs, but it slowed them a bit. Liz ended up going with a fast 200 DM, Halfer went for more mods. He had over 35 % combined GT/Temp/GN on day 11 or something like that. There were no really apparent other threats just yet, apart from one, so all we had to do at that point was to hang on and outlast the Halfer, and kill the next threat.


The next threat was a Lizardfolk in #23: This Is My Ninja Way. He turned out to be Kenshiro. He went for an extremely fast 500 DM and no mods and he kept up nicely while 9 and us were busy beating the stuffing out of each other. He ended up as our next FB target once the war with 9 was over and was promptly FBd down to 7k pez for about 24h. It took him another 18h to emerge on 19k peasants after he’d built FHs. He hadn’t been able to train a lot and his Keep was still increasing, so his peasants were huge at that point in the game. Lightning and I were playing around with 12-14k peasants at most at the time, so his lead was substantial. He did trail in OP a lot.


We switched to some Lightning Bolts, but those don’t do a lot early on, so Kenshiro wasn’t as down and out as we’d hoped. At this point, I’d completed my 400 Alchs and both lighty and I were starting on DM tentatively. Unfortunately, unlike the war with 9, we couldn’t get a war hit.


All this time, 9 Vhagar was pushing and pushing and pushing mods. He skipped building anything but mods and the essential smithies/farms etc, which meant a run-or-bust strat with his Halfer. He couldn’t stop and build because he’d fall behind. Even so, he was legitimately within one or two moves of running at all points, forcing lightning to train a lot of DP and forcing me to tail his sends. For a good bit of the second and third week, that was all we did: defend 9 halfer. However, all things must come to an end, and on day 18, Vhagar made his last hit, hitting #32 Goblin ladeda. After that, it took him 17 days to send again – and that was on a wonder too. Still, it was a very nicely played strat, albeit one that didn’t run. Good game. :)


A third threat emerged once we hit 2200a, right around the time 9 started to die off: 28 Blue Angel, whose Kobold converted with 20 % GT and an absolutely staggering 12 % temples. He went massive Underlings to hit up 1k acres in 4 days or something. By this time, Lighty had all-but overtaken Halfer and I’d grabbed MG to get on par. 28 was playing it very safe too. He could have suicided 9 at least twice by my count, and I’m sure lightning never was completely safe, but Blue Angel played it safe every time. He couldn’t hit one of us – he’d get doubled in return.


We ended up shifting our attention to 28. He had made two quick hits that left him on shaky all-in, and we sealed the deal with a snare + 2k Swords dump by lighty on day 20.


1:53:54 AM We have declared WAR on We have a wonder now :) (# 28)!

10:59:22 AM Victorious on the battlefield, Russian roulette (# 16) conquered 252 land from Blue Angel (# 28).


He also spent time trying to convince Lightning of not hitting him and doing a NAP for the win. Yeah, as if. Tetre also chipped in, saying 28 was his friend and that we shouldn’t hurt him. Again, no dice.


Oddly, the idea of training spies to get out of being snared just didn’t seem to be fashionable. 9 didn’t train any and 28 spent his time training Beasts and UL mainly. Only when we were nearly done with the entire war – on day 22 – did he start to train spies in great numbers. Too bad for him we still kept him snared until the end of the war early day 23.


The day we canceled the war, we missed that Kenshiro had huge OP freed up by Lightning hitting out of range by hitting #24 Merfolk earlier in the day. A little assassinating later and our Goblin – ImperioN – who was in absolutely hugely good shape, got hit.


4:59:45 AM Victorious on the battlefield, Russian roulette (# 16) conquered 202 land from Archimedes Death Ray (# 24)

3:28:30 PM This is My Ninja Way (# 23) invaded The Invadinator (# 16) and captured 306 land.


We weren’t pleased about that at all, but we soldiered on. This is also around the time when I ended up getting asked about #35 Chopped Ice, who had been hit three times by us in short succession. Truth be told, I have no idea who that was. All I know is that he explored into range of Lightning while being 15k DP short or something. The other hits on him were a little closer, but 4k drafts do not good DP make when there’s an exploring Halfling in your range and you’re only safe by 700 DP.


Day 25 was when I managed to get myself out of the race by bouncing on #25 Prince. Long story short, I rushed a send and I used wrong ops. I repeated myself on day 29 as well. Oops.


Day 27 was when Rhaegal in #9 finally played his sui-card. We’d been expecting it for some time, to be honest, but it still hurt. By this point, lightning had only two attackers in range: 25 Prince since I’d failed to knock him out and Kenshiro. The rest of the realm greatly profited and I managed to get a back to back hit on Chopped Ice, so all was well.


Lightning managed to get #9 Crystal on day 28 for some neat acres. Oddly, he hadn’t even started converting but he still had GN. More on that later…


1:59:57 PM Victorious on the battlefield, Russian roulette (# 16) conquered 310 land from Crystal (# 9).


Then on day 29. I bounced again, same target, same type of mistake and all that rot, and I ended up on the wrong end of a ~5k spy/wiz release into suicide by #28 Blue Angel.


11:10:40 AM Victorious on the battlefield, Blue Angel (# 28) conquered 405 land from slow.internet.guy (# 16).

11:12:19 AM The Prince (# 25) invaded Blue Angel (# 28) and captured 228 land.

11:13:12 AM Napoleon Bonaparte (# 16) invaded Blue Angel (# 28) and captured 228 land.

11:14:07 AM T r o l o l o (# 27) invaded Blue Angel (# 28) and captured 204 land.

11:15:52 AM Wolfen (# 2) invaded Blue Angel (# 28) and captured 173 land.

11:49:21 AM Mucho Badasstico (# 16) invaded Blue Angel (# 28) and captured 167 land.


This one made it onto the boards, I think. It also raised more than a few questions.


I managed to recover by hitting #29 Velveeta Village. Sauce wasn’t pleased, saying I could’ve hit any of the ones who hit #28 as well, but we were low on SS at that point after trying to get Viserion in 9 – he released enough to make me not hit him. Chances were I could’ve hit, but getting a good enough BS was going to be tricky on our low SS.


Fountain of Youth finally came out on day 32, which meant that we coordinated our way into sending on it. This boosted pop some more, but by this time the second tier had to start watching out as #29 Battle Rain was powering up the ranks on a very powerful 900 DM strat.


Skip to day 34 and we had another hit for Lightning and war.


2:59:57 AM Victorious on the battlefield, Russian roulette (# 16) conquered 288 land from GJHoldings Inc (# 18).

10:56:23 AM Golden Dragons (# 9) has declared WAR on us!


Tetre ended up being blackopped, but he doubled on #9 Meraxes to get out of range of the blackops. 9 ended up taking the Fountain eventually. Lightning also made a hit on day 35, saying goodbye to yet another exploring / ever-converting Kobold with GN:


12:59:57 AM Victorious on the battlefield, Russian roulette (# 16) conquered 356 land from Auf Wiedersehen (# 15).


Then the second incident that made it onto the boards this round. Kenshiro had been following Lightning for a few days, but by this point, he was falling behind hard and required AC to be safe all-in. Lightning dumped about 5k Swordsmen to catch him, and when Kenshiro noticed, he decided to turn the tables:


Day 36:

10:59:46 AM This is My Ninja Way (# 23) invaded Russian roulette (# 16) and captured 629 land.

11:00:32 AM Victorious on the battlefield, Russian roulette (# 16) conquered 335 land from This is My Ninja Way (# 23).

11:59:29 AM Victorious on the battlefield, slow.internet.guy (# 16) conquered 340 land from This is My Ninja Way (# 23).

12:00:00 PM Victorious on the battlefield, Mucho Badasstico (# 16) conquered 382 land from This is My Ninja Way (# 23).


Well played Kenshiro. I’ve heard cheating allegations, but fact remains that you were also playing a very nice round that only got superseded by our DE.


I also got hit again, this time by Battle Rain who sent 3600 Knights along, but with literally everyone out and Kenshiro scripted a few hours after sending, his 252k + incoming was sufficient to hit my 282k.


4:46:36 PM Battle Rain (# 29) invaded slow.internet.guy (# 16) and captured 440 land.


Kenshiro had also sent messages around to others, which meant that #25 Prince was around to profit. Since Lightning was still assuming it was an all-in and he was on phone, his Dominion revolted for sending over 5:4. This allowed Prince to sneak in a hit 2 sec before Lightning, to his detriment. He landed in range on about 15k DP short, which led to:


11:30:52 PM Victorious on the battlefield, Russian roulette (# 16) conquered 345 land from The Prince (# 25).


We replied to losing the Fountain by declaring war for Portals of Transportation, which negated the advantage that Lightning’s main competitor – Battle Rain – would have. Tetre also ended up slamming #9 Crystal twice on day 37:


4:01:16 AM We have declared WAR on realm 11 needs more lube, Ouch (# 1)!

6:26:39 AM Victorious on the battlefield, Napoleon Bonaparte (# 16) conquered 414 land from Crystal (# 9).

6:12:23 PM Victorious on the battlefield, Napoleon Bonaparte (# 16) conquered 287 land from Crystal (# 9).


Both hits were ACless. Just after we got Portals of Transportation on day 38, we noticed a build-up of spies and wiz on Battle Rain, when he already had enough to get ops on everyone in his range. Checking others revealed huge incoming spy/wiz and with their and our position being what it was, I assumed it was for blackops on Tetre, so we alerted him to that. It slowed him down quite a bit, but 29’s initial impact was blunted. They could’ve done a lot more damage had they warred on day 38 instead of day 39. I decided to unconvert and return the favour a bit: it was that or train up to 90 % DR and still not hit anyway.


Nothing much happened for a few days apart from blackops flying to and fro, and mostly to us. Well, Rush warred us on Day 41: 8:30:04 AM TREMBLE IN FEAR YOU GUYS (# 42) has declared WAR on us!


Then, Tetre managed another hit on a Kobold, also without AC, also one of those Kobolds doing the ever-converting strategy with some 150-200 GN - #12 Sunshine. I lost TC for this day, but it was 306a.


Then that one moment. Tetre made another ACless hit on #25 Prince – who’d trained to suicidal (337/278) OP, but apparently forgot AC all-in. He had intimated – 8hrs before his hit – that he had a hit lined up in 9h. Lightning decided that he would intentionally bounce on someone to give 29 a free shot at Tetre. It was the subject of loads of discussion and the point that 29 might snatch the win now was raised multiple times. In the end, it went through. We didn’t all agree on the decision, but we at least understood it. Erik summed it up best:


"Giving them free 100% hit is just plain stupid but kills a cheatzor"


Day 43:

10:58:15 AM Victorious on the battlefield, Napoleon Bonaparte (# 16) conquered 337 land from The Prince (# 25).

5:20:42 PM Sadly, the forces of Russian roulette (# 16) were beaten back by Forest Mirage (# 28).

5:40:59 PM Battle Rain (# 29) invaded Napoleon Bonaparte (# 16) and captured 504 land.

6:08:19 PM Velveeta Village (# 29) invaded Napoleon Bonaparte (# 16) and captured 467 land.

Day 44:

1:19:09 AM Sadly, the forces of WoodOfSteal (# 16) were beaten back by Velveeta Village (# 29).


Following this, the round heated up more as 29 became more and more powerful, allowing them to outtrain Lightning completely. Lightning held off on razing Velveeta too, which would’ve been possible after Daniel bounced because he forgot war wasn’t active any more. If done, this would’ve done about 2.5 – 3 % damage to all of his OP/DP, based on what we observed on the raze on #28. Lightning then found one last hit late day 45 on the ridiculously underdefended #19 Halfer:


11:59:48 PM Victorious on the battlefield, Russian roulette (# 16) conquered 399 land from genuit sacra lumina (# 19).


I believe he could leave 25 % of his OP at home.


Eventually, the round culminated in yet another war declaration by #29, which turned out to be for more OP and for Portals. We were set to go for a 3:2 scenario – it seemed the most likely with them banking to tank Lightning’s economy completely, when #14 took City of Gold. We hadn’t anticipated people warring for City on day 48 of all days. This freed up Portals to go to 29 and ended with me deleting to not be a 29 snack and Lightning releasing his OP. The rest is, therefore, second-hand information.


Apparently, Tetre messaged Lightning and said he may still save the round by suiciding if he got a public apology. Lightning wasn’t inclined to do that, so it didn’t happen. Battle Rain did leave himself open to a 5:4 suicide from #27 Troll – the same one he hit after Lightning released all his OP - and then another from Tetre. This didn’t happen and so the round was over. #27 said that he did not think it would affect the outcome and Tetre said he slept through his window of opportunity.


I don’t think it’d be a lie if I said that everyone in the pack is pretty gutted with how the last few days went down. Congratulations to #29, but you’ll forgive us if some of us aren’t too happy at the moment.
