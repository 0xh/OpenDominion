Here's my review of Round 55. Planning for this round started already two rounds earlier when i was thinking about an atteching strat with suicidal attacks to lose land and generate alot of researchpoints with multiple hits on small landsize. Because we had a round with Imp-ruleset i decided to skip it and play the Atteching strategy at a later point. And in Round 54 i just wanted to try for once a real converter after usually telling people that it is a good way to play without much risk. And i knew that Atteching would be best under Techrules anyway. Just by this time this Strategy was already made popular by Talium who played it already in R53 and R54 and also by Takao who played it as Troll in R54.

But both of them executed the strategy in a different way then i had in mind as i didn't want to go oop on 333 acres. Off course you have easy targets then but also a huge drawback on the economy. Because of that i went oop on 515 acres but i found that i was too larget to hit landdefense as the script was kicking in on the first dominion i wanted to hit. Luckily Rio invaded me only a few minutes after i left protection and suddenly i had really good landdef targets in range without any script making trouble. When wave 6 left protection i got hit another time but this was pretty annoying and on 480 acres i was no good target at all with 3k defense. But that was all defense i trained for some time then and went into full suicidal mode going straight for Magical Weaponry and shortly after Dark Artistry.

Takao went into the round with the same strategy, just he went oop on 333 and had more schools. So in the beginning he had a huge advantage on his techs while i had tons of Spirit Warriors more then he had. In the end it proved to be better going for economy over the early techs.

So with this "slower" teching i reached MW on day 7, giving me almost 17k offense on my 500-600 acres. This sped up my RP-generation alot and so techs begun rushing in faster and faster. The shortest average time between two techs was only 7 hours at one point. So by day 7 i had 7 techs and on day 11 i was at 16 techs. At this point i decided to stop building schools and starting my "conversion" to a normal growth mode. The main problem for this was going from no defense (around 5k maybe) to more then 15k defense to stay safe above 600 acres. I managed to get 10k defense on day 12 but i was still hit alot. But with careful choice of targets a bit earlier i was at maximum prestige at this point. Also i was lucky on my heroes as i was able to get all classes to level 6+. My Smith was the only class i got no second time during my 95 Eliteguard hits until the rounds end. If i had known he would be my only one i would have not risked to send him over level 6.But i was lucky with it and had a Early level 8 Smith with lots of other heroes. Only with my first alchemist i had no luck as he died on level 5. Later in the round i send heroes above 6 only if i had two of the class i wanted to send and stopped when one of them died as you can see on my 2 level 9 miners and level 8 healers. The boni from them surely helped alot, especially the smith who saved millions of plat in the end and also the miner who counted for 75 dmines for several weeks.

I did not pay much attention to the things happening above me but on day 12 there was some major stuff going on. The Dwarf in #54 and the Goblin in #9 got suspended. After that happened, Realm #5 declared War on us and started Fireballing Slater (Malady of the Mind) which slowed him a bit.

Day 13 i managed to get enough defense to stop invasions on me finally. After this point i got hit not a single time for the rest of the round, so my growth started on 706 acres that day with 661 prestige and 21 techs. Three days later i reached 1k acres. But at this time i reached the point when i needed more offense in addition to my defense (i didn't train a single point of offense since 2 or 3 days oop) and i had no luck with targets so i was without any new landgain for about 30 hours. With newly trained op my growth continued until i was stuck again only 200 acres larger on day 19. But i went on hitting on day 20 and this was also the first time i used Adepts on offense. With 35k op and 74k dp things went good for me.

On day 20 we got HCT which i found to be very annoying. It was pretty useless for us (well in the end our two Undeads could get conversion hits on other undeads with it) and #5 was declaring war on us to gain it with an additional 100 prestige.

Only on day 24 i grew slightly more then 300 acres but decided to make a small break then to train up more offense and defense as i was closing up to some guys with more serious offense. Also news spread that i was growing fast and Sauce even told other to watch out for me and that they should hit me if possible. Nati was so friendly to forward me that message:

That being said I'd like to continue with a summary of the round from my perspective. I hope to keep it short, having kept no journal should help with that; no promises though.

The pack was formed late in r54 mostly due to Pasi knowing mris/Sarmis and me having kept contact to Lorian all through the round. Even before changes were announced we were certain that DE and UD would be overpowered, DE more than UD, but UD's strength would unfold earlier than DEs. Sarmis, mris and Lorian were of great help with running lots of numbers, convincing me of those races' strength. I was actually set to play Orc when Sarmis changed his mind to a Lyc shrine/all-WW strat and one UD spot was left untaken. We agreed we couldn't possibly pass on having 2 UDs and since Pasi and I wanted to play same race/strat for sometime I switched to UD.

I simmed myself a wave2 UD with 1300 vamps, 130 progs and 940 ghouls on 600a (50 schools). The plan was to train only vamps/ghouls until I hit 20k ghouls, use vamps turtle whenever I needed it and see how good I'd be able to keep up while teching to Magical Weaponry. Magical Weaponry with improved WGs (mini-homes) does not only make UD a lot more efficient, it also provides almost complete immunity to black ops. It turned out I trained my first progeny on day 21 at which point I was still in an awesome spot land-wise, but I'm getting ahead of time.

OOP went pretty well, I hit twice leaving enough vamps to be decently safe. I think I was able to 11-hr (hit with the vamps that I left home before I gained acres) 2 or 3 times early on which helped a good deal. I pushed schools to 150 and started rolling in techs. From there on I kept hitting 75% every 12 hours looking to maximize converts whenever possible. Top OP was rather low at early stages allowing me to send decent offense for much longer and be bigger than I had expected.

  1. 5 lycs (Gothia/fingal) were obviously going to be troublesome, though not as much for me, because of being evil. When the NAPed dwarf and gobbo started to take turns pushing up top OP I was first forced to sit for more than 12hrs (one out of not more than 4 or 5 occasions IIRC), skipping on acres as well as converts. I think I squeezed in another EG range hit or two before being forced into BFing for the sake of converts. Dwarf/Gobbo were soon deleted allowing the lycs to feed on good aligned explorers that relied on the dwarf to keep the lycs at bay, as well as push out nothing but offense. I have no memories about how well I was keeping up land-wise, but I never fell behind a lot and kept hitting 50%+ for converts, always defending the lycs 4:3.

They eventually war'ed and fireballed me, but I started rolling in WGs to be safe from serious damage and the FBs soon ceased. I was 2 techs short of Magical Weaponry at that point. At some point (again my memory of the exact course of events fails me) we "stole" HCT, which #5 had been hitting a lot, from #0 so that they wouldn't be given the ability to raze me and I could still trade hits with them if neccessary. They took it from us shortly after, but I managed to hit bobbo (#47 kobbo) twice before it finally fell, one of the hits being my last 75% hit until the lycs would finally die. This was the only time I didn't completely defend fingal and Gothia, but I could have still hit them back if they went after me. 2.4k progenies off the kobbo seemed worth it to me.

HCT fell and I really didn't like to see it in the hands of my main competitors at that point, but that was mostly due to the misunderstanding that I believed they would be able to kill my vamps on defense. If I knew that wasn't the case I would have looked to trade hits with them a lot earlier, they were by far the best convert targets out there. In the meanwhile Rommel's realm had taken PoT and he was going strong with his 6hr-return, running a goblin with good imps. Lorian was also nearing full-teched status and started to prepare for growth, ie. train DP. I kept hitting 50%+ pretty much every 12hrs, with slight pauses to build the fullcost acres here and there. On all of my hits I always left lots of vamps home to defend #5's 4:3, I think my fullsend offense was about equal to what the lycs and Rommel had.

The round sailed on smoothly, everyone on top growing at roughly the same pace. I was working towards reaching full 25% WGs and my economy was starting to shine compared to the lycs who were not only less efficient but also had worse imps. Only Rommel had me worried because he sometimes managed to send without defending the lycs (6hrs-return), obviously a great thing if you can save the spec DP and keep your economy up. He soon grew to have respectable OP, about that of the lycs backed up with good imps and a slightly better economy than me. Lorian was beginning to catch up and he was a little worried when I sometimes sent just after the lycs, allowing Rommel to make a hit as well. But basically all top-attackers losing OP allowed me to BF 3 times in a row, while they all got only one hit, so I was gaining more than them, which I felt was worth it. Rommel proceeded to zone in lots of GN pushing his OP to suicidal regions, though I still expected him to train DP at some point.

Lorian closed in and eventually grew to around the same size as Rommel, I believe I already had a decent land-lead on the rest at this point. Rommel used his suicidal OP to all-in fingal, giving Lorian, fingal and Gothia free acres. Sadly enough for me I was sleeping at that time and didn't get my share because of guard. The lycs couldn't have been more dead than they were at this stage, with Lorian training insane amounts of OP/DP per hour. My numbers for OP were almost as good, but training progenies really took it's toll on my speed compared to him. After all I wasn't worried though since I had played it overly safe all round, knowing it was only a matter of time until he would come up strong. Time came and we took on #5 lycs: Lorian first all-in'ed fingal, who was out of my range after that and deleted 12hrs after the hit. Gothia tried to safe himself by entering away mode, but with Ares down he was easy prey for us a few hour after fingal was gone.

If we didn't already, we had the round locked up right then. I was looking at around 80k peasants at peak (damn 20k specs), Lorian maxed out at 100k, not too shabby for attackers. It was complete bottomfeeding-frenzy afterwards, hitting mostly at the very bottom of my range, while Lorian kept trying to hit as big as possible because he now had losses to face. In retrospect I should have probably looked to hit bigger than 40%, but I thought I might run out of targets in the end. Lorian closed the gap quickly but he had already agreed to tie when I asked him earlier, so it was all good. I feel a little bad for making him wait and skip hits as well as not trying to hit bigger, but we didn't expect to reach 11k anyway.

In conclusion, I'm obviously pleased with how this round turned out, double win for the pack with almost 4k acres lead to the next closest, can't really ask for more than that. Most things worked better than expected and I never really fell behind other attackers a lot despite teching to 7 techs. I'm well aware that this round offered nice circumstances for an UD with low early top OP, no strong dwarfs and very few fast attackers in general, but it still took a great amount of dedication and activity. Could have been easier if I discovered the HCT thing earlier, worked out fine nonetheless. Also surprised at how well bottomfeeding and especially building the full cost acres works, I expected that to slow me more.
