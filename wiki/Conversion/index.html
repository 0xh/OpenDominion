Conversion

From The Dominion Encyclopedia

Jump to: navigation, search

Conversion (not to be confused with Converting) is a very powerful ability that certain Spirit, Undead, and Lycanthrope units have. Basically, when you successfully attack an enemy dominion, some of their dead units will be converted into one of your race's units. In order for this ability to have any effect, you must attack a dominion with units that will die. As such, SPUD and Lycanthrope players should avoid attacking SPUDs as well as Firewalkers with high amounts of Phoenix defenders.

SPUD conversions

Spectres/Vampires

In the Base and Imp Rulesets, Spectres/Vampires have the ability to convert either elite DP (Ghosts/Progeny) or specialists (Banshees/Ghouls and Phantoms/Skeletons). In order to generate elite units from your enemy's fallen, you must attack dominions that are 65% of your size or higher. If you attack a dominion who is between 50% and 65% of your land size, then you will convert equal numbers of OP and DP specialists. Attacking a dominion below 50% of your size with Spectres/Vampires will result in no conversions.

The Tech Ruleset is slightly different, and ends up being much better for SPUD players. Spectres/Vampires will convert Ghosts/Progeny on targets from 50% and up. Attacking under 50% yields no conversions of any kind.

  • See Spirit & Undead conversion ranges.

Ghosts/Progeny

SPUD elite DP is also able to convert units, however, with only 2 OP, they should not be used to attack unless you are a few hundred OP short of breaking a dominion and can spare their DP. Ghosts/Progeny always convert equal numbers of OP and DP specialists, and will convert units from a dominion of any size.

Lycanthrope conversions

Garous

During the Base and Imp Rulesets, attacking with Garous will cause some of your enemy's casualties to be converted into Werewolves. However, in Tech rounds, the conversion ability is removed from Garous.

Werewolves

During a Tech round, Werewolves are the only Lycanthrope unit that can convert. They convert to Werewolves.

Lycanthropes will receive conversions from attacks on enemies of any landsize.

Parasitic Hunger

This is the racial spell available to Lycanthrope, Spirit, and Undead. It has the ability to increase your conversions by 50%, but may not always do this as will be explained by the conversion formula.

Conversion formula

There are two formulas that are used to determine how many converted units you will gain from an attack; whichever formula yields a lower result is the one used to determine your conversions.

  • (Enemy Casualties) * % (Land Size)
  • (Converting Units Sent) * (0.06) * (% Land Size) [if Parasitic Hunger is cast, (Converting Units Sent) * (0.09) * (% Land Size)]

Enemy Casualties refers to the amount of enemy troops that die as a result of your invasion. % Land Size is the number that results when you divide your target's land size by your land size (ie, if your target has 1500 acres and you have 2000 acres, then % Land Size = .75). Converting Units Sent is the actual number of Spectres/Vampires, Ghosts/Progeny, Garous, or Werewolves that are sent on your attack; only units that have the conversion ability count for this number (so do not include Garous in a Tech round or Spectres/Vampires if you are hitting a dominion under 50% of your size).

This formula tells you the following information:

  • Higher enemy casualties will result in higher possible conversions, lower enemy casualties will cap the amount of units that you can convert, and no enemy casualties will result in no conversions.
  • The relative land size of your target has a large influence on both formulas; hitting a 75% target will always yield higher conversions than bottomfeeding.
  • Higher percentages of converting units in your offense may give higher conversions, thus you should not train OP specialists in place of elite units with the conversion ability. Also, a Lycanthrope should never leave Garous at home - they have no turtle, so sending more than are required might increase your conversions without lowering your DP. Your own casualties will not increase by oversending.
  • Parasitic Hunger may not increase your conversions depending upon how many converting units are sent and how many enemy casualties there are.
